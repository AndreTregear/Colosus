# Persona: Elena Quispe

## Profile
- **Name:** Elena Quispe Huallpa
- **Age:** 45
- **Location:** Cusco, Peru (Centro Histórico, a 2 cuadras de Plaza de Armas)
- **Business:** Sabor Cusqueño — traditional Peruvian restaurant (picantería) specializing in Cusco regional cuisine
- **Products/Services:** Daily lunch menu (menú del día S/15-20), à la carte dishes (S/25-65), catering for tourist groups, delivery via Rappi and PedidosYa
- **Team size:** 8 employees (2 cooks, 1 sous chef, 2 waiters, 1 cashier, 1 dishwasher, 1 delivery coordinator)
- **Annual revenue:** ~S/480,000 (~$128K USD)
- **Language:** Spanish (native, Cusco dialect), Quechua (conversational — uses with market vendors), English (basic — tourism phrases)
- **Communication style:** Warm but firm, uses voice notes while cooking, lots of "mamita" and "papito" in messages, sends food photos constantly, mixes Quechua words naturally ("allinmi" = it's good, "ñuqa" = I)
- **Payment methods:** Cash (60%), Yape (25%), BCP transfer (10%), POS Visa/Mastercard (5% — mainly tourists)
- **Tax ID (RUC):** 10234567890
- **Tax regime:** Régimen MYPE Tributario
- **SUNAT obligations:** Boletas de venta (tourists, individual customers), Facturas electrónicas (corporate clients, travel agencies), monthly IGV declaration

## Daily Tasks (what Elena actually does every day)
1. Go to Mercado San Pedro at 5:30am to buy fresh ingredients — menu depends on what's available
2. Plan the day's menu based on market purchases and post it on WhatsApp status/Instagram
3. Prepare the menú del día (11am-3pm lunch rush is 70% of daily revenue)
4. Manage Rappi and PedidosYa incoming orders during lunch peak
5. Handle walk-in tourist groups (often 10-20 people, need quick allergen info)
6. Process 40-60 transactions per day (mostly cash + Yape)
7. Verify Yape payments and match to tables/orders
8. Coordinate evening service (6pm-10pm, lighter crowd, more tourists)
9. Track daily food costs vs. revenue (target: 30% food cost)
10. Close cash register and reconcile daily sales

## Weekly Tasks
1. Plan weekly specials based on seasonal ingredients
2. Coordinate with Rappi/PedidosYa for menu updates and pricing adjustments
3. Pay employees (weekly cash for kitchen staff, biweekly for waiters)
4. Restock non-perishable ingredients (rice, oil, spices) from Makro/Plaza Vea
5. Review food delivery platform ratings and respond to customer reviews
6. Update allergen matrix when menu items change
7. Reconcile cash register with bank deposits
8. SUNAT batch processing — emit boletas and facturas for the week
9. Schedule deep cleaning and maintenance (kitchen equipment)
10. Check reservation requests from travel agencies for upcoming tourist groups

## Monthly Tasks
1. SUNAT monthly IGV declaration (18%)
2. Régimen MYPE income tax advance payment (pagos a cuenta — 1% of net income)
3. PLAME payroll declaration and EsSalud contributions
4. Calculate food cost percentage by dish category
5. Negotiate with suppliers for next month's bulk purchases
6. Review Rappi/PedidosYa commission statements and reconcile payments
7. Plan seasonal menu adjustments (June-August high season = more tourist-friendly dishes)
8. Evaluate staff performance and schedule shifts for next month
9. Renew municipal operating license and health inspection paperwork (DIGESA)
10. Update photo menu for international tourists (English/Spanish bilingual)
11. Review monthly profit margins and compare vs. previous year

## Pain Points (what keeps Elena up at night)
- **Menu planning chaos:** Menu changes daily based on market availability — no system to track what she's serving or forecast demand
- **Allergen liability:** International tourists with celiac, nut allergies, lactose intolerance — one mistake could be a disaster. No systematic allergen tracking
- **Cash reconciliation nightmare:** 60% cash business — money gets lost between tables, tips, and market purchases. Can't tell if she's profitable until month end
- **Delivery platform commissions:** Rappi charges 25%, PedidosYa 22% — eats into already thin margins. Can't track true profitability per platform
- **Seasonal revenue swings:** June-August is packed (tourists), February-April is dead. No analytics to plan cash flow across seasons
- **Tourist group billing:** Travel agencies want facturas with specific details, payment on 30-day terms. Tracking who owes what is a mess
- **Language barriers:** English-speaking tourists need allergen info, menu descriptions, billing — Elena's English is limited
- **No reservation system:** Tourist groups call, WhatsApp, email — bookings get lost or double-booked
- **SUNAT compliance stress:** Confuses when to issue boleta vs. factura, fears fines
- **Food waste:** Over-buys at market because no demand forecasting, throws away 15-20% of perishables
