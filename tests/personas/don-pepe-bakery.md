# Persona: Don Pepe — Bakery Owner

## Profile
- **Name:** José "Pepe" Quispe Mamani
- **Age:** 52
- **Location:** Villa El Salvador, Lima, Peru
- **Business:** Panadería y Pastelería Don Pepe — neighborhood bakery + custom cakes
- **Products:** Daily bread (pan francés, ciabatta, integral), pastries (empanadas, cachitos), custom cakes for events (birthdays, weddings, quinceañeras, baptisms)
- **Team:** Pepe (owner/master baker), his wife Rosa (cakes/decoration), 2 helpers, 1 delivery guy on motorcycle
- **Daily production:** 500-800 breads, 100-200 pastries, 2-5 custom cakes
- **Monthly revenue:** ~S/18,000 (~$4,800 USD)
- **Language:** Spanish (native, informal Peruvian slang heavy), zero English
- **Communication style:** Very informal, short messages, lots of voice notes, uses diminutives ("pancito", "tortita"), emoticons not emojis, types with one finger
- **Payment methods:** Cash (60%), Yape (35%), BCP transfer (5%)
- **Tax ID (RUC):** Régimen MYPE tributario
- **Tech level:** LOW — uses WhatsApp only, no computer, does everything from phone

## Daily Tasks
1. Wake up 4am, start baking
2. Check WhatsApp orders that came in overnight
3. Confirm custom cake orders (flavor, size, design, delivery time)
4. Send price list photos to customers who ask
5. Manage delivery schedule (motorcycle delivery within Villa El Salvador)
6. Accept Yape payments throughout the day
7. Close register and count cash at 8pm
8. Respond to cake inquiries that come in evening/night

## Weekly Tasks
1. Buy ingredients at Mercado Mayorista (flour, sugar, eggs, butter)
2. Calculate how much he spent vs earned
3. Pay helpers (cash, weekly)
4. Plan custom cake schedule for next week
5. Post cake photos on WhatsApp Status

## Monthly Tasks
1. Pay rent for the local (S/1,200)
2. SUNAT simplified tax declaration (régimen MYPE)
3. Tally up the month's earnings (pen and paper currently)
4. Decide prices — flour prices change and he needs to adjust
5. Plan ingredient purchases based on upcoming events season

## Pain Points
- **Cake order chaos:** Gets 15-20 cake inquiries per week, loses track of who ordered what, when it's due, what flavor
- **No written records:** Everything in his head or scattered WhatsApp chats
- **Ingredient costs spike:** Flour went up 15%, doesn't know when to adjust prices
- **Can't track profit:** Has no idea which products make money and which don't
- **Delivery coordination:** Customer says "deliver at 3pm" but he has 4 deliveries that hour
- **Late-night messages:** Gets cake requests at 11pm, can't respond until 4am
- **Repetitive questions:** "¿Cuánto cuesta la torta de 1 kilo?" asked 10x per day
- **Cash management:** Most sales are cash, hard to track, sometimes helpers don't record sales
