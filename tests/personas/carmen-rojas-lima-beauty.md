# Persona: Carmen Rojas

## Profile
- **Name:** Carmen Rojas Mendoza
- **Age:** 29
- **Location:** Lima, Peru (Los Olivos, Av. Carlos Izaguirre — zona comercial)
- **Business:** Beauty Studio Carmen — beauty salon and mini-spa in a growing middle-class district of northern Lima
- **Products/Services:** Haircuts (S/25-45), hair coloring/highlights (S/80-200), manicure/pedicure (S/25-60), facial treatments (S/60-120), eyelash extensions (S/80-150), keratin treatments (S/150-250), makeup for events (S/80-150). Also retails beauty products: shampoos, treatments, tools (20% of revenue).
- **Team size:** 6 (4 stylists — 2 senior + 2 junior, 2 aestheticians — 1 facial specialist + 1 nail tech). Carmen does hair and manages everything.
- **Annual revenue:** ~S/360,000 (~$96K USD)
- **Language:** Spanish (native, Limeña — "asu mare", "ya pe", "qué roche")
- **Communication style:** Super expressive, heavy emoji user 💅✨💇‍♀️, Instagram Stories junkie (posts 10+/day), uses voice notes, responds fast but sometimes chaotic, mixes beauty industry English terms naturally ("balayage", "ombré", "nail art", "glow up")
- **Payment methods:** Yape (45%), cash (25%), BCP transfer (15%), POS Visa/MC (10%), gift cards/vouchers (5%)
- **Tax ID (RUC):** 10567890123
- **Tax regime:** Régimen MYPE Tributario
- **SUNAT obligations:** Boletas de venta (all clients), facturas (corporate events, product wholesale), monthly IGV, income tax advance

## Daily Tasks (what Carmen actually does every day)
1. Check Instagram DMs and WhatsApp for appointment requests (gets 20-40 messages/day)
2. Confirm today's appointments — send reminders via WhatsApp 2 hours before
3. Manage walk-ins vs. appointments — juggle the schedule when someone runs late or cancels
4. Do 4-6 hair appointments herself (coloring, cuts, keratin)
5. Supervise aestheticians — quality control on facials and nail services
6. Process 15-25 payments per day (mostly Yape)
7. Post Instagram Stories: before/after transformations, available time slots, product promos
8. Handle no-shows (2-4 per day 😤) — try to fill the empty slot last minute
9. Sell retail products to clients during their service
10. Close register and reconcile daily sales

## Weekly Tasks
1. Plan weekly Instagram content and promotions
2. Review and update appointment availability for next week
3. Restock salon supplies (hair color, developer, nail polish, skincare products)
4. Pay staff commissions (stylists earn 30-40% commission on services)
5. Reconcile weekly payments: Yape, cash, POS, transfers
6. Manage loyalty program — track points, send rewards
7. Follow up with no-show clients (gentle message, no judgment)
8. Update price list if product costs changed
9. SUNAT batch boleta processing for the week
10. Review Google/Instagram reviews and respond

## Monthly Tasks
1. SUNAT monthly IGV declaration
2. MYPE income tax advance payment
3. PLAME payroll declaration (for formally employed staff)
4. Calculate commission payouts per stylist
5. Inventory audit — salon products and retail stock
6. Analyze which services are most profitable
7. Plan monthly promotions ("Martes de Manicure", "Viernes de Facial")
8. Review no-show rate and evaluate deposit policy changes
9. Evaluate new products and treatments to add to menu
10. Plan staff training (new techniques, product certifications)
11. Review and update loyalty program tiers
12. Instagram analytics review — what content drives bookings?

## Pain Points (what keeps Carmen up at night)
- **No-show epidemic:** 15-20% of appointments are no-shows. Each empty hour = S/50-100 lost. No deposit system to discourage it.
- **Scheduling chaos:** Manages appointments via WhatsApp chat + a paper notebook. Double-bookings, forgotten appointments, no buffer time between services.
- **Commission tracking nightmare:** 4 stylists × different commission rates × varying service prices = Excel hell every week. Frequent disputes.
- **Inventory blind spots:** Doesn't know when hair color or retail products are running low until they're gone. Emergency runs to the beauty supply store waste time and cost more.
- **Instagram dependency:** 80% of new clients come from Instagram. Algorithm changes tank her visibility. No other marketing channel.
- **Gift card/voucher tracking:** Sells gift cards but tracks them in a paper notebook. Some get lost, some get used twice, no way to verify.
- **No client history:** Doesn't remember what color formula she used on a client 3 months ago. Recreating looks from scratch wastes time.
- **Cash flow timing:** Pays suppliers immediately but commission staff weekly. Product investment ties up cash.
- **Loyalty program manual management:** Stamp cards get lost. Clients claim "I had 9 stamps" with no proof. System is honor-based and leaking money.
- **No analytics:** Doesn't know her true revenue per chair-hour, best performing stylist, or which services to promote.
