# Persona: Carlos Medrano

## Profile
- **Name:** Carlos Medrano
- **Age:** 33
- **Location:** Lima, Peru (Miraflores)
- **Business:** MedranoCorp SAC — import/export between Peru and China
- **Products:** Electronics accessories (phone cases, chargers, cables) imported from Shenzhen; Peruvian superfoods (maca, quinoa, camu camu) exported to Guangzhou
- **Team size:** 5 employees (1 admin, 2 warehouse, 1 sales, 1 driver)
- **Annual revenue:** ~S/1.2M (~$320K USD)
- **Language:** Spanish (native), basic Mandarin, functional English
- **Communication style:** Direct, uses voice notes frequently, mixes Spanish and English tech terms
- **Payment methods:** Yape, BCP bank transfer, international wire (SWIFT)
- **Tax ID (RUC):** 20612345678

## Daily Tasks (what Carlos actually does every day)
1. Check WhatsApp messages from Chinese suppliers (WeChat → WhatsApp bridge)
2. Respond to customer inquiries about product availability and pricing
3. Check inventory levels in warehouse (Callao district)
4. Process 5-15 orders per day via WhatsApp
5. Confirm Yape payments by checking screenshots customers send
6. Coordinate with delivery driver for Lima metropolitan shipments
7. Update pricing based on USD/PEN exchange rate fluctuations
8. Respond to after-hours messages (customers message 7am-11pm)

## Weekly Tasks
1. Review weekly sales numbers and top-selling products
2. Place restock order to Shenzhen supplier via Alibaba/WeChat
3. Pay employees (weekly payroll for warehouse workers)
4. Reconcile bank account (BCP) with orders
5. Update product catalog with new items from China
6. Follow up with customers who inquired but didn't buy
7. SUNAT electronic invoicing (boletas/facturas) batch processing
8. Review shipping status of incoming containers from China
9. Meet with customs broker about pending shipments

## Monthly Tasks
1. SUNAT monthly tax declaration (IGV - 18% sales tax)
2. Income tax advance payment (pagos a cuenta)
3. PLAME payroll declaration to SUNAT
4. Inventory audit (physical count vs system)
5. Review P&L and cash flow
6. Negotiate with freight forwarder for next month's container
7. Update pricing strategy based on competitor analysis
8. Review and renew insurance (cargo, warehouse)
9. Send export documentation to Guangzhou buyer (Certificate of Origin, phytosanitary cert)
10. Plan next purchasing trip to Yiwu/Shenzhen (quarterly)

## Pain Points (what keeps Carlos up at night)
- **Payment verification hell:** Receives 20+ Yape screenshots daily, manually matching each to an order
- **Exchange rate risk:** Buys in USD/RMB, sells in PEN. A 3% swing can wipe out margin
- **Inventory blindness:** Doesn't know real-time stock levels until physically checking
- **SUNAT compliance:** Monthly IGV declaration is stressful, fears making mistakes
- **Chinese supplier communication:** Time zone difference (13 hours), language barrier
- **Cash flow crunch:** Pays supplier 30% upfront, receives customer payment 15-30 days later
- **Customs delays:** Container can sit in Callao port for 5-10 extra days
- **No analytics:** Makes gut decisions, doesn't know true profit per product
- **Customer follow-up:** Forgets to follow up with 60% of interested leads
