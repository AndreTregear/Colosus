# Persona: Roberto Huamán

## Profile
- **Name:** Roberto Huamán Tapullima
- **Age:** 50
- **Location:** Iquitos, Peru (oficina en Boulevard de Iquitos, frente al malecón)
- **Business:** Amazonas Adventures — jungle tour operator offering 1-7 day immersive Amazon jungle experiences. Operates since 2008.
- **Products/Services:** Day trips (Monkey Island, Pilpintuwasi), 2-3 day jungle lodge stays, 4-7 day deep jungle expeditions, ayahuasca retreat coordination, birdwatching specialized tours, corporate team-building jungle packages
- **Team size:** 8 (6 jungle guides — 3 bilingual English/Spanish, 2 boat operators who also guide, 1 cook; 2 office staff — 1 booking coordinator, 1 admin/accountant)
- **Annual revenue:** ~S/520,000 (~$138K USD) — highly seasonal
- **Language:** Spanish (native, selva dialect — "causa", "churre", "qué bacán"), English (functional — learned from years with tourists), some Kukama-Kukamiria phrases for cultural tours
- **Communication style:** Enthusiastic, uses LOTS of emojis 🌿🐒🦜, sends voice notes while on the river, shares photos/videos of wildlife constantly, responds at odd hours (tours start at 5am), WhatsApp is his LIFE — 90% of bookings come through it
- **Payment methods:** International wire/PayPal (40% — advance deposits from foreign tourists), cash USD (25% — on-arrival payments), Yape (15% — local clients), BCP transfer (10%), credit card via payment link (10%)
- **Tax ID (RUC):** 10456789012
- **Tax regime:** Régimen MYPE Tributario
- **SUNAT obligations:** Boletas (individual tourists), facturas (travel agencies, corporate), monthly IGV, income tax advance payments

## Daily Tasks (what Roberto actually does every day)
1. Check WhatsApp messages from potential clients worldwide (gets 15-30 inquiries/day in high season)
2. Respond to booking requests with tour options, pricing, and availability
3. Coordinate daily tour logistics: which guide goes where, boat assignments, lodge availability
4. Confirm next-day departures — send clients meeting point, packing list, weather update
5. Monitor weather conditions and river levels (rainy season = itinerary changes)
6. Process payments — verify PayPal receipts, wire transfers, cash collection
7. Post photos/videos from today's tours on Instagram and WhatsApp status
8. Handle on-trip emergencies (medical, weather, boat issues) via satellite phone/WhatsApp
9. Manage lodge supplies and food provisions for multi-day trips
10. Follow up with clients who inquired but haven't booked

## Weekly Tasks
1. Update availability calendar across all tour packages
2. Reconcile payments received (PayPal, bank, cash) vs. bookings
3. Pay guides, boat operators, and lodge staff (weekly cash payments)
4. Review and respond to TripAdvisor/Google reviews
5. Coordinate with partner lodges for upcoming multi-day bookings
6. Restock tour supplies (rubber boots, rain ponchos, first aid kits, mosquito nets)
7. Emit boletas/facturas batch for the week's completed tours
8. Update pricing if USD/PEN exchange rate shifts significantly
9. Follow up with travel agencies about group bookings
10. Maintain boats — fuel, engine checks, safety equipment

## Monthly Tasks
1. SUNAT monthly IGV declaration
2. MYPE income tax advance payment
3. PLAME payroll declaration for formal employees
4. Reconcile PayPal account with bank account (PayPal → BCP transfers)
5. Review seasonal pricing strategy (adjust for high/low season)
6. Negotiate with lodge partners for next month's rates
7. DIRCETUR tourism operator license renewal and compliance
8. Review cancellation/refund log and net revenue impact
9. Plan marketing: TripAdvisor ads, Instagram content calendar
10. Evaluate guide performance based on client reviews
11. Insurance renewal for boats and tour operations
12. Forecast next month's revenue based on current bookings pipeline

## Pain Points (what keeps Roberto up at night)
- **Booking chaos:** Manages everything via WhatsApp threads. Double-bookings happen when he's on a 4-day jungle trip with no signal. No centralized calendar.
- **Payment tracking nightmare:** Receives payments in 5 different methods across 3 currencies (USD, PEN, EUR). Reconciling who paid what deposit for which tour is a spreadsheet disaster.
- **Cancellation/refund headaches:** Weather cancellations (rainy season = flooded trails), sick tourists, no-shows. Each needs different refund handling. No clear policy enforcement.
- **USD pricing volatility:** Prices tours in USD but pays costs in PEN. Exchange rate moves can eat 10% of margin overnight.
- **Guide scheduling complexity:** 6 guides with different skills (bilingual, birdwatching specialist, ayahuasca-certified). Matching guides to tours while respecting rest days is manual juggling.
- **Seasonality cash flow:** May-October earns 75% of annual revenue. November-April is survival mode. No analytics to plan ahead.
- **No-signal zones:** On multi-day jungle trips, has no cell service for 2-4 days. Office staff must handle bookings independently.
- **Review management:** One bad TripAdvisor review can tank bookings for weeks. Can't monitor and respond in real-time from the jungle.
- **Safety liability:** Jungle tours have real risks (snakes, river conditions, medical emergencies). Documentation of safety briefings and waivers is paper-based.
- **Travel agency commissions:** Agencies take 15-25% but bring volume. Hard to know if net profit justifies the commission.
