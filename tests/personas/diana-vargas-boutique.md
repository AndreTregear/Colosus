# Persona: Diana Vargas — Online Fashion Boutique

## Profile
- **Name:** Diana Vargas Chávez
- **Age:** 27
- **Location:** Surco, Lima, Peru
- **Business:** Boutique Dianita — online fashion store (Instagram + WhatsApp)
- **Products:** Women's clothing (imported from China/Turkey), accessories, handbags, shoes
- **Model:** Dropship + small inventory. Posts on Instagram → customers order via WhatsApp DM → ships from her apartment or directly from supplier
- **Team:** Solo (Diana does everything — photos, social media, sales, packing, shipping)
- **Monthly revenue:** ~S/8,000 (~$2,100 USD) — growing fast
- **Language:** Spanish (native), conversational English for supplier communication
- **Communication style:** Very fast, lots of emojis 💕✨, uses abbreviations ("x fa" = por favor, "tb" = también), responds from phone constantly, sends product photos with prices overlaid
- **Payment methods:** Yape (80%), Plin (10%), BCP (10%)
- **Tax ID:** None yet (informal, considering formalizing)
- **Social media:** Instagram 12K followers, TikTok 5K, WhatsApp Business catalog
- **Shipping:** Olva Courier, Shalom, motorcycle delivery for Lima

## Daily Tasks
1. Post 3-5 products on Instagram Stories
2. Respond to 30-50 WhatsApp messages from interested buyers
3. Confirm orders and send payment instructions
4. Verify Yape/Plin payments (screenshots)
5. Pack and label orders for shipping
6. Drop packages at Olva Courier
7. Send tracking numbers to customers
8. Handle "¿todavía hay?" (is it still available) messages constantly
9. Update Instagram bio with "DISPONIBLE ✅" or "AGOTADO ❌"

## Weekly Tasks
1. Upload new product batch from supplier (photos, descriptions, prices)
2. Calculate weekly profit (revenue - product cost - shipping - packaging)
3. Restock popular items from supplier
4. Handle returns and exchanges
5. Plan Instagram content (reels, stories, posts)
6. Check what competitors are selling and at what price
7. Follow up with customers who asked but didn't buy

## Monthly Tasks
1. Count inventory (what's in her apartment closet-warehouse)
2. Calculate monthly P&L
3. Transfer savings to separate account
4. Plan next month's purchases from suppliers
5. Consider formalizing business (getting RUC, SUNAT)
6. Review which products sold well vs which are stuck inventory

## Pain Points
- **Message overload:** 30-50 WhatsApp messages daily, same questions ("hay stock?", "cuánto?", "envían a provincias?")
- **Stock confusion:** Sells same item on Instagram and WhatsApp, oversells items she only has 3 of
- **Payment verification fatigue:** Screenshots all day, manually matching each one
- **No pricing system:** Calculates profit mentally per item (cost + shipping + her margin)
- **Returns are painful:** Customer wants to return, but Diana already spent the money
- **Shipping logistics:** Tracking 15-20 packages daily across 3 courier services
- **Zero bookkeeping:** Has no idea of her actual profit margin
- **Sizing issues:** #1 return reason. Customers pick wrong size, blame her
- **Supplier delays:** Orders from China take 20-30 days, customers don't want to wait
