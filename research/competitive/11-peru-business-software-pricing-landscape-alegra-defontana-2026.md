# Peru Business Software Pricing Landscape: Alegra, Defontana, and the Micro-Enterprise Gap (2026)

**Research Document #11 (Competitive)**
**Date:** March 21, 2026
**Category:** Competitive Intelligence / Pricing Analysis
**Relevance:** Critical — maps the pricing floor and ceiling that Yaya competes against and reveals the massive gap between existing solutions and the informal micro-enterprise market

---

## Executive Summary

Peru's business software market in 2026 is paradoxical: there are dozens of accounting, invoicing, and ERP solutions available — yet 75.3% of micro-enterprises keep zero financial records (INEI 2024). The problem isn't supply; it's product-market fit. Existing solutions are designed for businesses that already operate formally, use computers, and have bookkeeping habits. Yaya enters a market where the "competition" isn't other software — it's paper notebooks, WhatsApp message history, and human memory.

This document maps every significant business software product available to Peruvian SMBs in 2026, their pricing, features, and target market — and demonstrates why none of them serve the 3.5M informal micro-enterprises that constitute Yaya's primary market.

---

## 1. The Peru Business Software Landscape: Overview

### 1.1 Market Structure

Peru's business software market segments into five tiers:

| Tier | Examples | Monthly Cost | Target | Peru Market Size |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Enterprise ERP** | SAP Business One, Oracle NetSuite, Dynamics 365 | $1,000–$5,000+/mo | Large companies (500+ employees) | ~2,000 companies |
| **Mid-Market Cloud ERP** | Odoo Enterprise, Defontana Corporativo | $300–$800/mo | Medium businesses (20–100 employees) | ~15,000 companies |
| **SMB Cloud Accounting** | Alegra, Siigo, CONCAR, Bsale, Defontana Starter | $13–$150/mo | Small businesses (1–20 employees) | ~200,000 companies |
| **Micro Invoicing Only** | Nubefact, Facturador SUNAT gratuito, Alegra Emprendedor | $0–$30/mo | Micro-enterprises needing SUNAT compliance | ~500,000 companies |
| **Nothing (paper/WhatsApp)** | Paper notebooks, calculator, WhatsApp history | $0 | Informal micro-enterprises | **~3,000,000 businesses** |

**The gap is obvious:** 85% of Peru's businesses (the informal micro-enterprise majority) use no business software at all. The existing software industry is optimized for the top 15%.

### 1.2 Total Market Revenue

Peru's SaaS and business software market is estimated at $180–250M annually (Statista 2025), but this is heavily concentrated in Lima, in formal businesses, and in companies with 10+ employees. The micro-enterprise segment contributes virtually nothing to this number — which means Yaya isn't taking share from existing vendors but creating a new market entirely.

## 2. Detailed Competitor Pricing (Verified February–March 2026)

### 2.1 Alegra (Colombia-founded, strong Peru presence)

**What it is:** Cloud accounting + invoicing + inventory + POS. The most popular SMB accounting platform in Peru.

**Peru-specific advantages:** SUNAT-integrated PSE/OSE (electronic invoicing provider), Spanish-language interface designed for LATAM, 15-day free trial, WhatsApp integration for sending invoices.

**Pricing (Peru, March 2026):**

| Plan | Monthly Price | Invoices/Month | Users | Key Features |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Emprendedor | S/49 (~$13) | 100 | 1 + contador | Basic invoicing, WhatsApp invoice sending, sales reports |
| PYME | S/89 (~$24) | 300 | 2 | + Inventory, purchase orders, payment tracking |
| Pro | S/149 (~$40) | 700 | 3 | + Bank reconciliation, recurring invoices, multiple warehouses |
| Plus | S/259 (~$70) | 1,500 | 5 | + API, custom roles, advanced inventory |
| Premium | Custom | Unlimited | Custom | Enterprise features |

**What Alegra does NOT do:**
- No voice input (requires manual typing in web/mobile interface)
- No AI-powered insights or recommendations
- No appointment scheduling or CRM
- No proactive engagement (user must log in deliberately)
- No WhatsApp-native workflow (WhatsApp is used only to *send* invoices, not to *manage* the business)
- Interface requires computer literacy and accounting vocabulary

**Target user:** Formal small business owner (5–20 employees) who already uses a computer, understands invoicing concepts, and has a contador who guides software adoption.

**Why Alegra doesn't reach informal micro-enterprises:**
- Requires web browser or app download
- Assumes user understands accounting concepts (débitos, créditos, IGV, categorías)
- Interface is text-heavy with tables, forms, and menus
- Onboarding requires configuring tax settings, chart of accounts, and business information
- No voice notes, no conversational interface
- Minimum useful plan (S/89) is expensive for businesses earning S/800–2,000/month

**Alegra's significance for Yaya:** Alegra proves the market exists at the S/49–89 price point, but its product design leaves the bottom 80% of businesses completely unserved. Yaya's Emprendedor tier (S/49) matches Alegra's entry price but delivers value through conversation, not forms.

### 2.2 Defontana (Chile-founded, Peru expansion)

**What it is:** Cloud ERP targeting structured small-to-medium businesses. More robust than Alegra but more complex.

**Pricing (Peru, February 2026, verified via Adra Tech Systems):**

| Plan | Monthly Price | Target |
|---|---|---|
| Starter | S/299 + IGV (~$93) | Small business with basic needs |
| Emprendedor | S/499 + IGV (~$155) | Small-medium business |
| Corporativo | S/799 + IGV (~$249) | Medium business with complex needs |

**3-year TCO: ~S/21,500 (Emprendedor plan)**

**Why Defontana is irrelevant for Yaya's market:** At S/299+/month, Defontana costs more than many informal micro-enterprises earn in profit. It's designed for businesses with 10+ employees, formal accounting practices, and computer-based workflows. Zero overlap with Yaya's target.

### 2.3 Siigo (Colombia-founded)

**What it is:** Administrative and accounting software, cloud-based, competing with Alegra at the SMB level.

**Peru presence:** Active but smaller than Alegra. Offers electronic invoicing with A4/A5/Ticket formats.

**Key differentiator vs. Alegra:** More traditional accounting focus; interface designed for accountants rather than business owners. Support hours limited (Mon–Fri, 8:30 AM–6 PM).

**Pricing:** Plans range from basic invoicing to full accounting. The "Pro" plan is required for most useful features — a common upsell pattern.

**Why Siigo doesn't reach micro-enterprises:** Same reasons as Alegra (computer-dependent, text-heavy, accounting-vocabulary-required) but with weaker UX and more limited support.

### 2.4 Bsale (Chile-founded, POS-focused)

**What it is:** Cloud POS (Point of Sale) + invoicing. Popular with retail stores and restaurants.

**Peru relevance:** Active in Lima, especially with restaurants and small retail. Integrates with SUNAT for electronic boletas and facturas.

**Pricing:** S/99–S/299/month depending on features and terminals.

**Why Bsale doesn't reach micro-enterprises:** POS systems assume a fixed retail location with a screen/tablet. Most informal micro-enterprises (salon owners, market vendors, service providers) don't have a traditional "point of sale" — they sell through conversation, often via WhatsApp. Bsale serves a different behavior pattern entirely.

### 2.5 CONCAR (Peru-native)

**What it is:** Traditional Peruvian accounting software. One of the oldest in the market. License-based (not cloud SaaS).

**Pricing:** S/150/year license fee. Configuration is do-it-yourself.

**Target:** Accountants managing multiple small businesses. Not designed for business owners directly.

**Significance:** CONCAR demonstrates that Peru's accounting software market has historically been "built for contadores, not for business owners." This is a key distinction — Yaya is built for the business owner, with the contador as a secondary beneficiary.

### 2.6 SUNAT Facturador Gratuito

**What it is:** SUNAT's free electronic invoicing tool. Basic web interface for generating CPE (Comprobantes de Pago Electrónico).

**Cost:** Free.

**Limitations:** Extremely basic interface, requires manual entry of every field, no inventory management, no reporting, no mobile optimization, no integration with anything, frequent downtime. Used by micro-enterprises who have no other option but are required to issue electronic invoices.

**Significance for Yaya:** The existence of SUNAT's free tool means Yaya's invoicing feature competes against "free" — but the UX is so poor that any improvement is dramatic. Yaya's conversational invoicing ("Factura a Juan, 3 camisas a S/45") versus SUNAT's 15-field web form is a 10× improvement in experience.

### 2.7 Nubefact (Peru-native, PSE/OSE)

**What it is:** Electronic invoicing provider (PSE + OSE). API-first approach for integrating SUNAT invoicing into other systems.

**Pricing:** From S/70/month for 500 documents. Per-document cost ~S/0.14.

**Significance for Yaya:** Nubefact is the integration partner (documented in technology/06), not a competitor. But its pricing sets the floor for invoicing costs — Yaya can offer invoicing within its S/49 subscription with healthy margins.

### 3.2 The "Value Per Cognitive Dollar" Framework

Traditional business software requires significant "cognitive investment" — learning to navigate, understanding accounting terms, remembering to log in and enter data. For users with limited digital literacy, this cognitive cost is often higher than the financial cost.

**Cognitive cost comparison:**

| Product | Financial Cost | Cognitive Cost | Value Delivered | Net Value |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Paper notebook | S/2 | Very low (familiar) | Minimal (no analysis, easy to lose) | Slightly positive |
| Alegra Emprendedor | S/49/mo | High (new interface, accounting terms, login habit) | Moderate (reports, invoicing) | Negative for low-literacy users |
| **Yaya Negocio** | **S/49/mo** | **Very low (talk to WhatsApp)** | **High (AI insights, proactive summaries, invoicing)** | **Strongly positive for all literacy levels** |

**This is Yaya's core competitive advantage in Peru:** Same financial price as Alegra, but 10× lower cognitive cost, making the net perceived value dramatically higher for the target user.

## 4. Competitor Vulnerabilities and Yaya's Strategic Position

### 4.1 Alegra's WhatsApp Feature Is Superficial

Alegra advertises "Facturación desde WhatsApp" as a feature, but this means:
- Sending a pre-formatted invoice PDF to a client via WhatsApp
- Receiving payment notifications via WhatsApp

It does NOT mean:
- Managing the business through WhatsApp conversation
- Voice input for data entry
- AI-powered analysis via chat
- Proactive business insights delivered via WhatsApp

Alegra uses WhatsApp as a *distribution channel* for documents. Yaya uses WhatsApp as the *operating system* for the business. The difference is fundamental.

### 4.2 None of These Products Use AI Meaningfully

As of March 2026:
- **Alegra:** "Reportes inteligentes" = pre-formatted charts, not AI-generated insights
- **Defontana:** No AI features mentioned
- **Siigo:** No AI features
- **Bsale:** No AI features
- **CONCAR:** Legacy software, no AI

Yaya's AI capabilities (natural language understanding, voice transcription, pattern recognition, predictive insights) are genuinely novel in this market. No existing Peru business software can understand a voice note like "Vendí 3 cortes a S/30 y 2 tintes a S/50, todo en efectivo" and extract structured data from it.

### 4.3 The Contador Channel Is Underexploited

Contadores manage 20–50 SMB clients each. They're the kingmakers of business software adoption in Peru. Yet existing products treat contadores as users, not as channel partners:

- **Alegra:** Offers a "Contador" plan for accountants — but it's a management dashboard, not a referral program with economic incentives
- **Defontana/Siigo:** No structured contador channel programs
- **CONCAR:** Used directly by contadores, but contadores don't earn from recommending it

Yaya's proposed 20% recurring commission for contadores (documented in strategy/09) would be the first economically incentivized accountant channel in Peru's SMB software market. This is a structural competitive advantage.

## 6. Pricing Strategy Implications for Yaya

### 6.1 The S/49 Anchor Is Validated

Alegra's Emprendedor plan at S/49/month proves that Peruvian micro-enterprises will pay this amount for business software. Yaya doesn't need to educate the market on pricing — Alegra has already established the anchor.

### 6.2 The Free Tier Is Yaya's Unique Weapon

No existing competitor offers a meaningful free tier for micro-enterprises:
- SUNAT Gratuito is free but terrible UX and invoicing-only
- Alegra's 15-day trial expires
- Defontana/Siigo have no free options

Yaya Básico (free, 50 invoices/month, basic tracking) fills a genuine market gap. It serves the 75% of businesses that currently keep zero records. Even if only 3–5% convert to paid, the free tier generates:
1. Transaction data (valuable for embedded finance)
2. Network effects (clients get reminded via Yaya → awareness)
3. Social proof ("I use Yaya for my business" → peer adoption)
4. Behavioral habit formation (daily usage → eventual upgrade)

### 6.3 Consider a S/29 Tier

The INEI data (market/07) suggests that at S/860/month average income for formal micro-enterprise workers, S/49 represents 5.7% of monthly income — somewhat high for a discretionary business tool. A S/29 tier (3.4% of income) could dramatically expand the conversion funnel:

| Tier | Price | % of Monthly Income | Target |
|---|---|---|---|
| Básico | S/0 | 0% | Habit formation, data collection |
| **Lite (proposed)** | **S/29** | **3.4%** | **Value-conscious micro-entrepreneurs** |
| Negocio | S/49 | 5.7% | Committed business owners |
| Pro | S/149 | 17.3% | Growing businesses, multiple employees |

A S/29 tier with 150 invoices/month and basic insights could be the "impulse purchase" tier that captures the most price-sensitive segment while still being profitable at 85%+ gross margins.

## 7. Key Takeaways for Andre

1. **You're not competing against Alegra — you're competing against paper notebooks.** 75% of your target market uses zero business software. The relevant comparison is "paper vs. Yaya," not "Alegra vs. Yaya."

2. **At S/49, you match Alegra's price but destroy it on accessibility.** Voice input, zero computer requirement, WhatsApp-native — this is 10× lower cognitive cost for the same financial cost.

3. **No competitor has meaningful AI.** "Reportes inteligentes" in existing products means pre-formatted charts. Yaya's ability to understand voice notes and generate insights is genuinely novel in Peru.

4. **The contador channel is wide open.** No competitor offers economically incentivized referral programs for accountants. Yaya's 20% commission program would be a first-mover advantage.

5. **Consider adding a S/29 "Lite" tier.** At 3.4% of monthly income, this is true impulse-purchase territory for necessity entrepreneurs earning S/800–1,500/month.

6. **The free tier is strategic, not charitable.** It generates data, habits, and network effects that feed the entire business model — especially the embedded finance layer.
