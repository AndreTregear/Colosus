## PART 4: RECOMMENDED EXPANSION SEQUENCE

### Phase 1: Peru Saturation (Months 0-18)

**Target:** 50,000-100,000 users across Lima + top 5 cities

| Quarter | Focus | Target Users |
|---------|-------|-------------|
| Q2-Q3 2026 | Lima beauty salons (beachhead) | 500-2,000 |
| Q4 2026 | Lima multi-vertical (food, retail, social sellers) | 5,000-10,000 |
| Q1 2027 | Arequipa + Trujillo expansion | 15,000-25,000 |
| Q2-Q3 2027 | Cusco, Piura, Chiclayo + all verticals | 30,000-50,000 |

### Phase 2: Colombia (Months 12-24)

**Why first:** Highest composite score (92/100). DIAN tax skill already built. 94% WhatsApp penetration. 5.18M micro-enterprises. Nequi/Daviplata payment infrastructure mature. Same language (minor dialect adaptation).

**Entry point:** Medellín (startup culture, easier iteration) → Bogotá (volume) → Barranquilla/Cali

**Target:** 20,000-50,000 users in first 12 months

### Phase 3: Mexico (Months 18-30)

**Why second:** 5.23M micro-enterprises. 93% WhatsApp. SAT tax skill built. 3-4x Peru's market. But 83.8% still cash-dominant, and CFDI is the world's most complex e-invoicing system.

**Entry point:** Guadalajara or Monterrey (less competitive than CDMX) → CDMX (volume)

**Target:** 30,000-100,000 users in first 12 months

### Phase 4: Ecuador + Dominican Republic (Months 24-36)

**Ecuador:** Dollarized (no FX risk), geographic/cultural proximity to Peru/Colombia, SRI e-invoicing mandatory. Entry via Guayaquil.

**Dominican Republic:** 88.6% internet, 82% use WhatsApp for shopping, e-invoicing deadline May 2026 creates urgency. Tourism economy = high micro-enterprise density.

### Phase 5: Chile + Costa Rica + Panama (Months 30-42)

**Premium cluster:** Highest GDP per capita, best infrastructure, highest formality rates. Higher willingness to pay. Dollarized (Panama) or stable currency (Chile). Smaller markets but highest ARPU.

### Phase 6: Brazil (Months 36-48)

**The big one:** 14.6M MEIs, 99% WhatsApp, PIX dominance, SEFAZ skill built. But requires Portuguese localization, strong local competition (Take Blip, Zenvia), and complex state-level tax system. Brazil alone is larger than the rest of LATAM combined for this product.

### Phase 7: Argentina + Paraguay + Rest (Months 42+)

Argentina only when macro stabilizes. Paraguay/Bolivia/Central America as organic expansion follows.

**Defer indefinitely:** Venezuela, Honduras.

## PART 6: WHO TO TARGET FIRST — The Decision

### Option A: Stay the Course — Beauty Salons (Current Plan)

**Pros:** Proven beachhead, 40x ROI, clearest pain point, lowest closure rate, beautiful case studies
**Cons:** Only 47K-50K in Peru (small SOM), 1.5-2M LATAM-wide, risk of being perceived as "salon software"
**Verdict:** Safe, validated, but may limit story for investors

### Option B: Pivot to Social Sellers (Largest Segment)

**Pros:** 4.7-10M businesses LATAM-wide (growing 42%/yr), already 100% digital, highest virality, no geographic constraint
**Cons:** Very fragmented (no single vertical pain), lower loyalty (sellers churn businesses frequently), harder to define ICP
**Verdict:** Massive TAM, but harder to nail PMF

### Option C: Multi-Vertical from Day 1 (Recommended)

**Pros:** Industry-specific skills already built (bodega, restaurant, salon, agro). Open-source model means users self-select their vertical. Hardware distribution works for ALL verticals.
**Cons:** Diluted focus, harder to tell one clear story
**Verdict:** The open-source model naturally supports multi-vertical. Let the market self-select. Track which verticals have highest activation/retention and double down.

### Recommendation

**Launch multi-vertical but MEASURE by vertical.** The open-source + Baileys model means any micro-business owner can install and use Yaya. Don't artificially constrain to salons. Instead:

1. **Build the platform to be vertical-agnostic** (which it already is via skills)
2. **Create vertical-specific onboarding flows** (salon skill, bodega skill, restaurant skill, social seller skill)
3. **Measure activation/retention by vertical** in the first 90 days
4. **Double down marketing** on whichever vertical shows highest daily engagement (VNPUAD >3)
5. **Tell the story of the winner** — if salons activate best, lead with salons. If social sellers do, pivot the narrative.

The hardware distribution model (laptops/USBs) especially benefits from multi-vertical — a USB with Yaya works for any business type in any market.
