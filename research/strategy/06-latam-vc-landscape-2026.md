## 1. Executive Summary

Latin America's venture capital market in 2025 deployed $4.126 billion across 681 deals — a 13.8% increase in capital but the fewest deals since 2017. The message is unambiguous: **investors are writing bigger checks to fewer companies**. Average ticket size climbed 16% to $6.1M. Peru received just $35M across 8 rounds — the smallest allocation in the region, below even Uruguay ($40M). Yet Peru's startup investment grew 28% in 2025 (PECAP), and AI startups are reaching revenues in 6 months that previously took years. For Yaya Platform, this landscape creates both constraints and opportunities that directly shape fundraising strategy.

The early-stage crunch also validates the seedstrapping approach: raise minimal capital, reach revenue fast, then raise seed from a position of proven traction.

## 3. The 2026 Investor Mindset

### 3.1 What Investors Want Now

Based on interviews with LATAM VC professionals (BVC, Cuantico VP, The Startup VC):

1. **Revenue traction, fast.** "Companies are reaching revenues in six months that they never used to reach... companies barely a year old already hitting $100,000 in revenue" — Ana María Prieto Peña, BVC.

2. **Capital efficiency.** "The pendulum is beginning to swing back — 2026 and 2027 should bring more liquidity, but dealflow is far from what the industry needs" — José Kont, CEO Cuantico VP.

3. **Defensible AI moats, not wrappers.** Investors are "cautiously observing AI startups" and worrying about whether "it's going to be replaceable." Yaya's local LLM infrastructure + SUNAT integration + transaction data moat directly addresses this concern.

4. **Evidence over narrative.** "PowerPoint isn't being paid for anymore: evidence is" — Sustainabl trialogue. The 15% 3-year survival rate means investors demand validated unit economics before writing checks.

5. **Regional ambition.** "8 of the top 10 Peruvian startups are expanding beyond Peru" (Gestión). Investors want Peru-origin companies with LATAM scale potential.

### 3.2 The AI Funding Paradox

AI startups in LATAM face a specific paradox: they can monetize faster than traditional startups, but investors question sustainability.

**The concern:** "Things are happening too fast. You have to be more cautious about what they're really building, and whether it's going to be replaceable" — Prieto Peña, BVC.

**Yaya's answer:** The product is not just an AI wrapper — it's a full business management stack with SUNAT compliance integration, transaction data accumulation, and embedded finance roadmap. The AI is the interface; the moat is the data and integrations.

### 3.3 The Seedstrapping Trend

The rise of "seedstrapping" is directly relevant to Yaya's situation:

- **Definition:** Raise a small seed round, reach revenue fast, skip or delay Series A
- **Why it works in 2026:** AI-native tools lower the capital required to launch; no-code/low-code stacks reduce engineering headcount needs
- **LATAM validation:** AI startups are achieving $100K revenue in <12 months with small teams
- **Yaya fit:** Local LLM infrastructure (already built on c.yaya.sh) dramatically reduces cloud API costs; WhatsApp as the interface eliminates app development costs; Nubefact handles invoicing compliance

**Recommended approach:** Seedstrap to $5K MRR (200+ paying users), then raise $250–500K seed round. This gives Yaya leverage: "We already have traction. We want capital to accelerate, not to discover product-market fit."

## 5. Fundraising Timeline & Strategy

### Phase 0: Seedstrapping (Months 1–9)

**Goal:** 200+ paying users, $5K+ MRR, validated unit economics
**Funding:** Founder savings ($20–30K) + early revenue
**Activities:**
- Build core product
- Launch beauty salon pilot
- Validate Sean Ellis score (>40%)
- Document unit economics meticulously

### Phase 1: Seed Round (Months 9–12)

**Raise:** $250–500K on SAFE or convertible note
**Valuation:** $2–4M pre-money
**Target investors:** BVC, NXTP, Bossa Invest, DOMO.VC, EWA Capital
**Use of funds:** Product acceleration, Colombia entry, team (2–3 engineers)
**Pitch narrative:** "97% of Peruvian entrepreneurs use WhatsApp for business, but zero companies combine AI + full ERP in that channel. We have 200+ paying users at 93%+ gross margin."

### Phase 2: Series Seed/Bridge (Months 18–24)

**Raise:** $1–3M (if needed)
**Valuation:** $8–15M pre-money
**Trigger:** 2,000+ paying users, 3+ countries, payment processing revenue live
**Target investors:** Monashees, Redpoint Eventures, Quona Capital
**Alternative:** Skip to Series A if growth trajectory supports it; or continue seedstrapping if unit economics are strong enough

### Phase 3: Series A (Months 24–36)

**Raise:** $5–10M
**Valuation:** $30–50M pre-money
**Trigger:** $200K+ MRR, 8,000+ paying users, embedded finance launched
**Target investors:** Regional growth funds, sector-specific fintech/AI funds
**Pitch narrative:** "We've built the conversational ERP category in LATAM. 8,000 businesses manage their operations through Yaya. Our embedded finance layer has turned transaction data into a credit scoring engine serving the $23.7B Peruvian SME credit gap."

## 6. Peru's Startup Ecosystem: Strengths & Weaknesses

### Strengths

- **28% growth in startup investment in 2025** (PECAP) — ecosystem momentum
- **Strong emerging startups:** YaVendió ($850K, AI sales), Equip ($1.4M, construction), Juztina ($500K revenue, legaltech), Leasy ($28M, vehicle fintech)
- **Active early-stage funds:** BVC, EWA Capital, Salkantay Ventures, UTEC Ventures
- **University ecosystem:** PUCP, UPC, Universidad de Lima, Universidad de Piura all running innovation programs
- **Regional expansion pattern:** 8/10 top Peruvian startups expanding to Mexico, Brazil, Colombia, Chile
- **SUNAT digitization push** creates natural demand for business software

### Weaknesses

- **Only $35M in VC (0.8% of LATAM total)** — tiny market; most capital goes to Brazil/Mexico
- **Only 8 deals in 2025** — very few companies get funded
- **No LATAM unicorn from Peru since Crehana** — limited exit track record
- **Regulatory barriers:** "Chile and Peru have their own barriers" for investment structures
- **Small angel network** compared to Mexico or Colombia
- **Brain drain:** Many Peruvian founders move to Mexico City or São Paulo for better funding access

### Yaya's Positioning Within Peru

Yaya can be a **"best of Peru"** story: a platform that solves a deeply Peruvian problem (97% WhatsApp business usage, 86% informality, SUNAT compliance) with technology that scales across LATAM. The fact that Peru's ecosystem is small means less competition for investor attention. BVC specifically invests in Peru, Colombia, and Brazil — Yaya's exact expansion path.

## 8. Key Takeaways for Andre

1. **Peru is the worst-funded LATAM market for startups** ($35M, 8 deals). This means you must build traction first — no one funds ideas in Peru. But a Peruvian startup with proven traction stands out in a thin pipeline.

2. **Seedstrapping is the right initial strategy.** The 2026 LATAM VC landscape rewards revenue-first companies. AI-native tools (your local LLM) and WhatsApp (zero app development cost) make this feasible with $20–30K personal capital.

3. **Target BVC and NXTP as primary seed investors.** BVC invests in Peru/Colombia/Brazil B2B startups at pre-seed/seed — perfect alignment. NXTP has the strongest LATAM syndicate network.

4. **The AI + fintech intersection is the hottest space.** Fintech captures 61% of LATAM VC; AI is the #1 investment thesis for top VCs (Bossa, DOMO.VC, Redpoint). Yaya sits at the intersection of both.

5. **The early-stage crunch creates a supply squeeze.** Pre-seed funding dropped 40%. Fewer startups getting funded today means less competition for seed/Series A in 2027–2028. Timing is favorable.

6. **Revenue velocity is the key metric.** LATAM AI startups are hitting $100K revenue in <12 months. If Yaya can hit $5K MRR by Month 9, it's in the top percentile of Peruvian startups and highly fundable.

7. **Plan for LATAM from Day 1.** 8/10 top Peruvian startups are expanding regionally. Investors expect it. Build the SUNAT integration in a way that's extensible to CFDI (Mexico) and DIAN (Colombia).
