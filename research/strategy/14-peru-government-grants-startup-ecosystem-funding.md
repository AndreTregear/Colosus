## 2. PROINNOVATE / STARTUP PERÚ: THE ANCHOR PROGRAM

### 2.1 Program Overview

ProInnóvate is Peru's flagship government innovation program, operated under the Ministry of Production (PRODUCE). Since 2014, it has:

- **Invested over S/190 million** (~$51M) across 12 generations of the StartUp Perú program
- **Funded 1,160+ startups** across all regions of Peru
- **Deployed S/19 million** in 2025 alone across its entrepreneurship portfolio
- Operates as **cofinancing with non-reimbursable capital** — no equity dilution, no debt

The program is now in its **13th generation (13G)**, launched in late 2025 with postulations closing January 16, 2026.

### 2.2 StartUp Perú Categories Relevant to Yaya

**Category 1: Emprendimientos Innovadores (Innovative Ventures)**
- **Eligibility:** Teams of 2-4 people OR companies with <18 months of operation and <S/120,000 annual revenue
- **Funding:** Up to **S/67,000** (~$18,000) in non-reimbursable seed capital
- **Requirements:** Must have a Minimum Viable Product (MVP) with market traction
- **Cofinancing:** Founder must contribute a matching portion (cash or in-kind, typically 20-50%)
- **Yaya fit:** ★★★★★ — Perfect for Phase 1 if Andre is pre-launch or very early

**Category 2: Emprendimientos Dinámicos (Dynamic Ventures)**  
- **Eligibility:** Formally constituted companies, 1-5 years old, with annual revenue between S/120,000 and S/1,200,000
- **Funding:** Up to **S/150,000** (~$40,000) in non-reimbursable seed capital
- **Requirements:** Must have innovative products already generating sales
- **Cofinancing:** Matching contribution required
- **Yaya fit:** ★★★★☆ — Ideal for Phase 2 (post-PMF, when revenue is flowing)

### 2.3 What ProInnóvate Actually Provides Beyond Cash

Based on alumni interviews (Startup Grind panel, January 2026):

1. **Capital semilla no reembolsable** — The cash, obviously
2. **Aceleración técnica y mentoría** — Structured mentorship from ecosystem experts
3. **Validación de mercado** — Market validation support and marketing assistance
4. **Investor visibility** — Improved profile with VCs; ProInnóvate alumni have higher follow-on funding rates
5. **Government credibility signal** — "Backed by PRODUCE" carries weight with institutional partners (banks, SUNAT, etc.)
6. **Network access** — PECAP conferences, demo days, alumni network

### 2.4 Application Strategy: Lessons from 13G Winners

From the Startup Grind panel (January 2026) featuring four ProInnóvate winners:

**What evaluators actually look for:**
1. **Clear problem validation** (40% of evaluation score) — Not just an innovative solution, but proof that customers will pay for it. Winners emphasized: "Innovation is good, but you need customers who buy your innovation"
2. **Scalability** (30%) — Demonstrate regional/global market potential
3. **Team composition** (20%) — Multidisciplinary teams score higher. Having technical + business expertise is critical
4. **Sustainability** (10%) — Show how the venture continues beyond the grant period

**Common mistakes that get projects eliminated:**
- Presenting a solution without a clearly defined problem
- Weak or incomplete legal structure (especially for fintech — "the legal part is crucial")
- Not demonstrating traction with real users/customers
- Applying solo — team-based applications strongly preferred
- Ignoring cofinancing requirements

**The "resilience signal":**
Multiple winners reported being rejected in earlier generations before winning. Evaluators value founders who show persistence — rejected in 11G, improved and won in 12G. "If you haven't been accepted in one batch, come back stronger for the next one."

**Tactical tips:**
- Pass through an incubator or accelerator first (Emprende UP, UTEC Ventures, Google for Startups) — it adds credibility
- Prepare for pointed legal questions if operating in fintech or regulated space
- Evidence of international traction/interest significantly helps in the Dinámicos category
- The jury presentation is high-pressure; practice handling "trap" questions

## 3. PERU'S ANGEL INVESTOR & VC ECOSYSTEM

### 3.1 PECAP: The Gateway Organization

The **Asociación Peruana de Capital Semilla y Emprendedor (PECAP)** is the non-profit association representing seed and venture capital investors in Peru. Key facts:

- **$257 million invested** in Peruvian startups over the past three years (2022-2024)
- Peru was one of only three LATAM countries (alongside Argentina and Colombia) to see VC investment **increase** in 2024 despite regional challenges
- Organizes the **Peru Venture Capital Conference (PVCC)** — 10th edition held October 2025, 1,200+ attendees, 200+ investors, 500+ entrepreneurs
- Regional expansion: **Perú Regional VC** events in Cajamarca (July 2025), bringing the ecosystem beyond Lima

**Why PECAP matters for Yaya:** Direct access to Peru's entire investor network. Andre should attend PVCC 2026 (likely October) and consider joining PECAP's startup community.
