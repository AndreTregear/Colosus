## 1. Executive Summary

Yaya Platform's seedstrapping model requires extreme capital efficiency in hiring. This document maps the LATAM AI/engineering talent landscape, cost structures, hiring models, and a phased team-building roadmap aligned with Yaya's business milestones. Key finding: **a production-ready founding team of 3–4 people (including Andre) can be assembled for $8,000–15,000/month in LATAM**, delivering capabilities that would cost $30,000–50,000/month in the US. The talent arbitrage is real, but retention, quality vetting, and cultural fit require deliberate strategy.

## 2. LATAM Engineering Talent: The 2025–2026 Landscape

### 2.1 Market Overview

Latin America has emerged as a premier nearshore talent market for US/global startups:

- **54% growth** in tech talent since 2018 (Torc research)
- **150,000+ STEM graduates annually** from Brazil and Mexico alone
- Major hubs: Mexico City, Guadalajara, São Paulo, Buenos Aires, Medellín, Bogotá, Lima, Santiago
- **30–70% cost savings** vs. US hiring at comparable senior-level quality
- Near-perfect timezone overlap with US East Coast (crucial for real-time collaboration)
- Remote-first culture accelerated by COVID — LATAM engineers expect distributed work

### 2.3 The AI Engineer Premium

AI engineers command a 20–40% premium over standard backend engineers in LATAM:

- **Mid-level AI Engineer:** $3,000–5,500/month (Simera 2025 benchmark)
- **Senior AI Engineer:** $5,500–8,000/month
- **Skills in demand:** LLM integration, RAG, vector databases, MLOps, Python, prompt engineering
- **Supply:** Growing but competitive — companies like Amazon, Google, Mercado Libre, and Globant actively poach LATAM AI talent

### 2.4 Peru-Specific Talent Market

Peru offers the most cost-effective option in LATAM for Yaya's founding team:

- **Lower salary floors** than Mexico, Colombia, or Brazil (20–40% less)
- **Growing tech ecosystem:** Lima now has a meaningful startup scene (Platanus, Endeavor Peru, Start-Up Peru)
- **Cultural alignment:** Team members understand the target market natively
- **Language:** Native Peruvian Spanish speakers can validate product copy, UX, and voice AI
- **Risk:** Smaller talent pool for specialized AI/ML roles — may need to hire regionally
- **Opportunity:** USD-denominated contracts are extremely attractive given sol devaluation trends

## 3. Hiring Models: What Works for Seedstrapping

### 3.1 Model Comparison

| Model | Cost | Control | Speed | Best For |
|-------|------|---------|-------|----------|
| **Freelancer/Contractor** | Lowest (hourly) | Low | Fast (days) | Specific tasks, prototyping, short-term |
| **Staff Augmentation** (agency) | Medium ($45–85/hr incl. margin) | Medium | Medium (1–2 weeks) | Scaling specific capabilities |
| **Direct Hire** (via platform) | Medium (salary) | High | Medium (2–4 weeks) | Core team members |
| **EOR/PEO** (Deel, Remote) | Higher ($300–600/emp/mo overhead) | Full | Slow (2–4 weeks) | Long-term employees with benefits |
| **Co-founder/Equity** | Lowest cash | Highest | Slowest | Mission-critical roles |

### 3.2 Recommended Model for Yaya Phase 1

**Hybrid approach:**

1. **Andre = founder/CTO/product.** Does architecture, core product decisions, AI integration, customer zero. No cost (founder equity).

2. **1 Senior Full-Stack Engineer (Peru):** Direct contractor hire at $4,500–6,000/month. Handles WhatsApp API integration, backend, database, deployment. Paid in USD via Deel or direct transfer.

3. **1 Mid-Level AI/NLP Engineer (Peru or Argentina):** Contractor at $3,000–5,000/month. Handles LLM prompt engineering, voice note processing (Whisper integration), conversational flow design.

4. **1 Part-Time Designer/UX (Peru):** Contractor at $1,000–2,000/month (part-time). Handles WhatsApp message templates, onboarding flow design, marketing materials.

**Total monthly team cost: $8,500–13,000** (plus Andre's living expenses at $1,500/month from strategy/08).

### 3.3 Why Not Hire Full Employees Yet?

During pre-revenue/early-revenue (Months 1–9):
- **No legal entity yet** — Peru SAC formation takes 2–4 weeks and creates ongoing obligations
- **Flexibility is crucial** — can scale team up/down as product evolves
- **No benefits overhead** — contractors in Peru don't require formal benefits
- **Equity conversations later** — prove the model works, then offer equity to convert key contractors

### 3.4 Hidden Costs to Budget

| Cost | Monthly | Notes |
|------|---------|-------|

## 6. Retention Strategies for LATAM Teams

### 6.1 Why People Leave

- **Better offers from MNCs** (Amazon, Google, Mercado Libre pay 50–100% more)
- **Inflation-driven churn** in Argentina (USD contracts mitigate this)
- **Lack of equity/upside** (contractors feel like mercenaries)
- **No career growth** (working for a 3-person startup with unclear future)
- **Burnout** (startup pace without startup equity)

### 6.2 Retention Toolkit

| Strategy | Cost | Impact | When to Deploy |
|----------|------|--------|----------------|
| **USD-denominated pay** | $0 extra | High | Always (standard for LATAM contractors) |
| **Equity grants** (phantom or real) | Dilutive | Very high | Month 6+ for key contributors |
| **Performance bonuses** | 10–15% of salary | High | Quarterly after Month 9 |
| **Professional development stipend** | $100–200/mo | Medium | From hire date |
| **Co-working reimbursement** | $100–150/mo | Medium | Optional |
| **Equipment upgrade** | $500–1,500 one-time | Medium | At hire + annually |
| **Mission alignment** | $0 | Very high | Cultural — ongoing |
| **4-day work weeks** (experimental) | $0 | High | If productivity allows |

### 6.3 The Mission Advantage

Yaya has a genuine retention advantage over generic outsourcing work: **the team is building something that helps real people in their own community.** A Peruvian engineer working on Yaya is building a tool for Rosa the salon owner down the street, not optimizing ad clicks for a Fortune 500. This matters. Lean into it.

### 7.3 Hiring Process Recommendation

For each key hire:
1. **Define role clearly** — JD with specific technical requirements + Yaya context
2. **Source from 2–3 channels** simultaneously (LinkedIn + platform + referral)
3. **Technical assessment** — Practical task (e.g., "build a WhatsApp message parser that extracts appointment details from Spanish voice note transcriptions")
4. **Cultural fit interview** — Does this person care about SMBs? Do they understand the market? Can they work autonomously?
5. **1-week paid trial** ($500–800) — Real project work before committing
6. **Formal contracting** via Deel or direct agreement

## 8. Key Risks and Mitigations

### 8.1 Talent Competition from Big Tech

- **Risk:** Amazon, Google, Mercado Libre, Globant all hiring aggressively in LATAM
- **Mitigation:** Compete on mission, equity, flexibility, and autonomy — not salary. A senior engineer at Amazon Lima earns $8,000–12,000/month but works on internal tools they'll never use. At Yaya, they're building the product and getting equity.

### 8.2 Single Points of Failure

- **Risk:** In a 3-person team, losing one engineer is catastrophic
- **Mitigation:** Code documentation, pair programming, shared context. Andre must be able to pick up any team member's work within 48 hours. No hero engineering.

### 8.3 Remote Team Coordination

- **Risk:** Distributed contractors may lack cohesion
- **Mitigation:** Daily async standups (voice notes in a team WhatsApp group — eating your own dog food), weekly video sync, shared Notion/Linear board, co-working days in Lima if possible.

### 8.4 Contractor Classification Risk

- **Risk:** Peru labor law could reclassify long-term contractors as employees
- **Mitigation:** Use Deel or similar EOR if contractor works exclusively for Yaya for 6+ months. Alternatively, formalize as Peru SAC employees after seedstrap raise.

The phased approach — solo founder → first engineer → AI specialist → designer — aligns team growth with business milestones and funding availability. Finding someone who is technically excellent, culturally aligned, and motivated by the mission (not just the paycheck) is worth spending 3–4 weeks on. Get this hire right, and everything else accelerates.
