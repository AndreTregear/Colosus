## 1. LATAM VC Market State: The Numbers

### 1.1 Capital Flow Summary

| 2021 | $17.4B | ~1,000+ | ~$17M | **Peak**: 35 mega-rounds, 22 unicorns |
| 2022 | $7.9B | ~900 | ~$8.8M | First correction: -55% |

## 2. Investor Preferences in 2026

### 2.1 What LATAM VCs Now Require

Based on the Cuantico VP report and founder surveys:

1. **Revenue traction** — Repeatable sales, not projections
2. **Unit economics** — Strong margins, controlled CAC
3. **Founder track record** — Serial entrepreneurs got 42% of 2023-2024 capital (vs 23% in 2021)
4. **Governance** — Clean cap tables, formal reporting
5. **Capital efficiency** — Low burn, fast path to breakeven
6. **AI with vertical application** — Generic AI is out; vertical AI with paying customers is in

### 2.2 Founder Sentiment

- **53%** of founders describe fundraising as "unfavorable" or "very unfavorable"
- **24.5%** took 6-12 months to close their round
- **30.6%** were still in process at time of survey
- Warm introductions are essential — cold outreach converts at very low rates

### 2.3 What Investors Are Excited About

From Ecosistema Startup analysis:
> *"Los inversores abandonaron el interés en plataformas de IA genéricas para enfocarse en aplicaciones verticales con casos de uso concretos: IA aplicada a salud, derecho, logística y productividad empresarial."*

Translation: Investors moved away from generic AI platforms toward **vertical AI with concrete use cases**. Yaya's positioning as "vertical AI for SMB business management" is perfectly aligned with this trend.

### 4.1 The Seedstrap Model (Validated by Market Data)

**Why it works in 2026:**
- Pre-seed/seed investors want capital efficiency, not cash burns
- Peru's $35M market means fewer competing deals for investor attention
- Local LLM infrastructure ($130/month) dramatically reduces cloud costs
- Solo founder proving unit economics before hiring = exactly what VCs want to see

**Why it's challenging:**

## 5. Fundraising Tactics for Peru's Tiny Market

### 5.1 Work Around Peru's 8-Deal-Per-Year Limit

Peru's $35M/8 deals in 2025 means the local VC market is extremely small. Tactics:

1. **Apply to YC or other accelerators**: Even a 2% YC acceptance rate is better odds than Peru's 8 annual deals
2. **Target Chilean/Colombian seed funds**: Chile ($249M, 53 deals) and Colombia ($224M, 62 deals) have much larger ecosystems and geographic proximity
3. **Position as "LATAM" not "Peru"**: Investors want regional scale. Lead with the 40M TAM, not the Peru beachhead
4. **Leverage Vambe's raise**: "Vambe raised $14M for WhatsApp AI commerce. We're the micro-business version — 100× the addressable market."
5. **Build in public**: Document the journey on X/Twitter and LinkedIn to create warm intro paths

### 5.2 Seedstrapping vs. Traditional Seed

The data supports Andre's seedstrap model:

| Metric | Traditional Seed | Seedstrap |
|---|---|---|
| Typical raise | $1-3M | $250-500K |
| Dilution | 15-25% | 5-15% |
| Time to close | 6-12 months | 2-4 months (smaller checks) |
| Investor expectations | 10× in 5-7 years | Revenue growth + optionality |
| Use of funds | Hiring + marketing | Hiring 1-2 key people |
| Path to breakeven | 18-36 months | 6-12 months |

The seedstrap is faster to close (smaller checks from fewer investors), preserves more equity, and aligns with the "capital efficiency" narrative investors want in 2026.

### 5.3 Alternative Capital Sources

Given Peru's limited VC ecosystem:

1. **Revenue-based financing**: Once Yaya has consistent MRR, platforms like Pipe, Clearco, or LATAM equivalents can provide non-dilutive capital
2. **Venture debt**: Growing in LATAM, especially for fintech (Yaya's embedded finance roadmap qualifies)
3. **Strategic partnerships**: Nubefact revenue-share could provide working capital without dilution
4. **Government grants**: Peru's INNOVATE program, Startup Peru, CONCYTEC
5. **Angel syndicates**: Lima's growing angel community (PUCP, UNI alumni networks)

## 6. Key Data Points for Andre

1. **LATAM VC hit $4.1B in 2025** — recovering but still 76% below the 2021 peak. Capital goes to fewer, stronger companies. Flight to quality.

2. **Peru got only $35M across 8 deals** — the smallest major market. Don't rely on Peruvian VCs alone. Target Chilean, Colombian, and global funds.

3. **Fintech captures 61% of all LATAM VC capital**. Position Yaya as "vertical AI + fintech" to access the largest capital pool. The embedded finance roadmap is not just a revenue strategy — it's a fundraising strategy.

4. **Average seed ticket rose to $1.73M**. Your $250-500K seedstrap is below average but within range. The advantage: faster close, less dilution, more control.

5. **First-time founders are disadvantaged** (repeat founders got 42% of capital). Counter this by proving unit economics before raising — the 138-paying-user breakeven target is your strongest fundraising asset.

6. **Vambe's $14M validates your thesis without competing with you**. Use it in every pitch: "They proved WhatsApp AI commerce works. We're serving the 100× larger micro-business market they can't reach."

7. **AI vertical applications are what investors want** in 2026 — not generic AI. Yaya's invoicing + inventory + SUNAT compliance focus is exactly the "concrete use case" investors are funding.

8. **Fundraising takes 6-12 months**. Start building relationships with investors NOW, even pre-product. Warm intros through the Lima tech ecosystem (PUCP, Platanus, startup events) are essential.
