## 1. THE ACQUISITION CHALLENGE

Yaya's Phase 1 goal is 500 active beauty salon users in Lima within 12 months. The beauty salon market in Peru is large (S/10B industry, 80K-120K salons), but acquisition must be hyper-efficient given the seedstrapping model (total pre-raise budget: ~$15K). This playbook maps the specific channels, tactics, and sequences for acquiring beauty salon customers in Lima's key districts.

### Target Salon Profile (ICP)

| Attribute | Ideal Customer | Why |
|---|---|---|
| Size | 1-5 employees | Owner-operator manages via WhatsApp |
| Location | Miraflores, San Borja, Surco, San Isidro, Barranco | Higher income, tech-forward, willing to pay |
| Services | Hair + nails + facials | Multi-service = more appointments = more value from Yaya |
| Monthly revenue | S/5,000-S/25,000 | Can afford S/49/month (1-2% of revenue) |
| Current tools | WhatsApp + paper notebook | No existing software = zero switching cost |
| Pain point | No-shows, payment collection, scheduling chaos | Yaya's core value proposition |
| WhatsApp usage | Heavy — manages client communication via WhatsApp | Already on the platform |

### Salon Owner Persona: "Rosa"

- **Age:** 35-55 (Gen X are biggest beauty spenders at S/426/year per COPECOH data)
- **Gender:** 51% of micro-enterprises are women-owned (INEI)
- **Education:** Secondary or vocational beauty training
- **Tech comfort:** Uses WhatsApp fluently for personal and business; smartphone-native but not app-native
- **Decision style:** Trusts peer recommendations (confianza); skeptical of cold outreach; responds to demonstrated value
- **Financial mindset:** Careful with recurring expenses; needs to see ROI within 30 days
- **Language:** Coastal Peruvian Spanish with possible Andean-Coastal influence; uses diminutives, "seño", "pe"

## 2. CHANNEL STRATEGY: ZERO-COST TO LOW-COST

### 2.1 Channel 1: Customer Zero (Andre's Own Business) — Cost: $0

**The Strategy:** Andre operates Yaya to manage a real business in Lima, creating living proof.

**Execution:**
1. Andre uses Yaya daily for a real business operation (even a small side business — tutoring, consulting, or a salon partnership)
2. Document everything: screenshots, metrics, before/after comparisons
3. Create "Caso de éxito #1" content: real numbers, real savings, real voice notes
4. Share in beauty industry WhatsApp groups and social media

**Why this matters:** Peru has the lowest interpersonal trust in LATAM (54% low trust). The founder-as-Customer-Zero strategy is the strongest possible trust signal. Rosa doesn't trust ads — she trusts someone who uses the product themselves.

**Timeline:** Month 1 onward (continuous)

### 2.2 Channel 2: Click-to-WhatsApp Instagram Ads — Cost: $5-15/lead

**The Strategy:** Run targeted Instagram ads to Lima salon owners that open a WhatsApp conversation (not a landing page).

**Why Click-to-WhatsApp (CTWA):**
- All WhatsApp messages within the 72-hour CTWA window are **completely free** (Meta's July 2025 pricing revolution)
- No landing page to build; no app to download; conversation starts instantly
- Rosa's Instagram feed is full of salon content — she's already there
- Instagram ad targeting can reach "beauty salon owner" + Lima + 25-55 age range

**Ad Creative:**
```
📱 ¿Cansada de que tus clientas no lleguen a su cita?

Yaya es tu asistente en WhatsApp que:
✅ Envía recordatorios automáticos
✅ Reduce las faltas hasta un 50%
✅ Te dice cuánto vendiste hoy

GRATIS por 30 días. Escríbeme por WhatsApp 👇
```

**Targeting:**
- Location: Lima Metropolitana (then narrow to Miraflores, San Borja, Surco, Barranco)
- Interests: Hair salon, Beauty salon, Cosmetología, Peluquería
- Age: 25-55
- Gender: All (12% of salon clients now male — male barbers are a segment)
- Behaviors: Small business owners, WhatsApp Business users

**Budget:** $300/month × 6 months = $1,800 total
**Expected results:** At $5-15 CAC for CTWA ads, 120-360 conversations initiated over 6 months
**Conversion funnel:** Conversation → Free trial activation (60%) → 30-day usage → Paid conversion (10%)

**Timeline:** Month 2 onward

### 2.3 Channel 3: Beauty Supply Distributor Partnerships — Cost: $0

**The Strategy:** Partner with beauty product distributors (L'Oréal Professionnel, Wella, Kativa) who visit salons weekly.

**Why distributors:**
- L'Oréal Professionnel and Wella each have 24% of Peru's professional hair care market (Euromonitor)
- Distributors visit 20-50 salons per week — they're the trusted intermediary
- Salon owners already have a commercial relationship with them
- Distributors benefit: if salons grow revenue, they buy more product

**Execution:**
1. Identify top 3-5 beauty product distributors serving Miraflores/San Borja
2. Offer them a partnership: for every salon they refer that activates Yaya, they get S/10 Yape credit
3. Provide them with printed QR code flyers (links to Click-to-WhatsApp)
4. Monthly check-in: "¿Cuántas salones nuevas esta semana?"

**Cost:** S/10/referral × 100 referrals = S/1,000 ($270)
**Expected quality:** High — distributor-referred leads have built-in trust

**Timeline:** Month 3 onward

### 2.4 Channel 4: Beauty School Partnerships — Cost: $0

**The Strategy:** Partner with cosmetology schools in Lima to introduce Yaya to graduating students.

**Why beauty schools:**
- 120 new beauty companies entered Peru in 2025 (3× typical rate)
- Graduating stylists are starting their own salons — they need business tools from Day 1
- Schools include: Pivot Point Peru, Montalvo Institute (25 locations), SENATI beauty programs
- Students are younger, more tech-forward, and forming habits now

**Execution:**
1. Offer free workshops: "Cómo manejar tu salón desde WhatsApp" (20-minute demo)
2. Give graduating students 3-month free Yaya access
3. Create a "Graduate Starter Kit" with Yaya + SUNAT registration guide + Culqi payment setup

**Cost:** $0 (Andre's time)
**Expected results:** 20-50 graduates per quarter; 30% activation rate = 6-15 new users/quarter

**Timeline:** Month 4 onward

### 2.5 Channel 5: Accountant Partner Program — Cost: 20% Revenue Share

**The Strategy:** Partner with accountants who serve beauty salon clients.

**Why accountants:**
- Each accountant serves 20-50 SMB clients
- They're the trusted business advisor for many micro-entrepreneurs
- Yaya solves their problem too: 75.3% of MYPEs keep zero financial records (INEI)
- If their clients use Yaya, the accountant gets cleaner data

**Execution:**
1. Identify 10-20 accountants in Miraflores/San Borja who serve beauty/personal care businesses
2. Offer 20% recurring commission on referred clients (S/9.80/month per client)
3. Provide accountant-specific dashboard: client revenue summaries, SUNAT compliance status
4. Monthly accountant meetup: "Red de Contadores Yaya" — share best practices

**Economics:** At 20% commission on S/49 = S/9.80/month. Accountant with 10 clients earning S/98/month passive income — meaningful in Peru.

**Timeline:** Month 5 onward

### 2.6 Channel 6: Salon Owner WhatsApp Groups — Cost: $0

**The Strategy:** Join and create value in existing salon owner WhatsApp groups.

**Why WhatsApp groups:**
- Peru's beauty professionals network heavily through WhatsApp groups
- APEB (Asociación Peruana de Empresarios de la Belleza) coordinates through WhatsApp
- Groups exist for: district-level salon owners, beauty product buyers, cosmetology graduates
- 41-57.5% of WhatsApp message volume is from group chats (SQ Magazine, 2025)

**Execution:**
1. Don't spam. Provide genuine value first: share industry statistics, SUNAT deadline reminders, business tips
2. After building trust (2-4 weeks), share Rosa's case study organically
3. Offer to demonstrate Yaya in a 5-minute voice note walkthrough
4. "¿Quién quiere probar? Mándame un mensaje directo"

**Cost:** $0 (Andre's time)
**Conversion rate:** Low volume but highest quality — peer-referred leads have 3-5× higher conversion

**Timeline:** Month 1 onward (continuous)

## 3. ACQUISITION FUNNEL METRICS

### 3.1 The WhatsApp-Native Funnel

```
Awareness (Instagram ad, QR code, referral, group mention)
  ↓ Scan/Click → Opens WhatsApp conversation
First Message (Yaya greets, asks about salon) ← 72h free window
  ↓ 60% continue conversation
Onboarding (5-message flow: name, salon name, services, schedule)
```

## 4. ONBOARDING FLOW: THE FIRST 5 MINUTES

The onboarding experience is designed for someone who has never used business software:

### Message 1 (Yaya → Rosa):
> ¡Hola! 👋 Soy Yaya, tu asistente de negocios en WhatsApp. Voy a ayudarte a organizar tu salón y a que nunca pierdas una cita.
> 
> ¿Cómo te llamas?

### Message 2 (Rosa → Yaya):
> Rosa

### Message 3 (Yaya → Rosa):
> ¡Mucho gusto, Rosa! 😊 ¿Cómo se llama tu salón?

### Message 4 (Rosa → Yaya):
> Salón Bella Rosa

### Message 5 (Yaya → Rosa):
> ¡Qué lindo nombre! 💇‍♀️ ¿Qué servicios ofreces? Puedes mandarme una nota de voz si prefieres.

### Message 6 (Rosa → Yaya):
> [Voice note: "Corte de cabello, tinte, manicure, pedicure, tratamientos capilares..."]

### Message 7 (Yaya → Rosa):
> Perfecto, ya tengo tus servicios. Ahora lo más importante: ¿quieres que le mande un recordatorio a tu próxima clienta? Pásame su nombre y número, y yo me encargo.
> 
> Así de fácil funciona Yaya ✅

**Design principles:**
- Never ask for email, password, or app download
- Accept voice notes from message 1
- Deliver value (first reminder) within the first conversation
- Use diminutives and Peruvian warmth ("¡Qué lindo nombre!")
- Keep it to <10 messages before first value delivery

## 6. SCALING: FROM 100 TO 500 SALONS

### 6.1 Network Effects in Beauty

Beauty salon networks have natural viral mechanics:

1. **Product sharing:** When Rosa tells clients "Te mando el recordatorio por WhatsApp", the client experiences Yaya. If the client is also a salon owner (or has a friend who is), viral loop activates
2. **Supply chain:** Rosa's colorist, nail tech, or product supplier sees Yaya working and asks about it
3. **District clustering:** Miraflores salons talk to each other. One success story spreads to the entire district
4. **Beauty school alumni networks:** Graduates share tools that work

### 6.2 Expansion Sequence

| Phase | Districts | Target Salons | Strategy |
|---|---|---|---|
| Month 1-3 | Miraflores, San Borja | 100 | Direct outreach + CTWA ads |
| Month 4-6 | Surco, San Isidro, Barranco | 150 | Distributor referrals + word-of-mouth |
| Month 7-9 | La Molina, Magdalena, Jesús María | 100 | Accountant channel + organic |
| Month 10-12 | San Juan de Lurigancho, Comas, Los Olivos | 150 | Proven playbook + lower-cost positioning |

### 6.3 District Economics

Districts 1-3 (A/B socioeconomic): Higher ARPU potential (S/79-149), more tech-forward, easier to acquire
Districts 4+ (C/D socioeconomic): Larger volume, lower ARPU (S/49), higher price sensitivity but larger market

**Important:** Grupo Montalvo (25 locations, 12% growth) reports their average ticket went from S/100 to S/120 in 2025. Young clients went from 1% to 6%, male clients from 6% to 12%. The market is expanding and diversifying — not just traditional female clientele.

## 7. COMPETITIVE RESPONSE PREPARATION

### 7.1 If AgendaPro (Chile) enters Peru

AgendaPro is the closest competitor at $19-299/month with 20K+ businesses. If they target Peru:
- **Yaya's defense:** Lower price (S/49 vs. $19+), WhatsApp-native (vs. separate app), voice input, Yape/Plin integration, cultural fluency
- **Counter-narrative:** "AgendaPro te pide descargar otra app. Yaya funciona directo en tu WhatsApp."

### 7.2 If a new local competitor appears

- **Speed advantage:** By Month 6, Yaya will have real usage data and testimonials that a new entrant can't replicate
- **Network advantage:** Accountant and distributor channels create relationship lock-in
- **Data advantage:** 6 months of salon transaction data enables AI features (predict busy days, suggest optimal scheduling) that competitors can't match without similar data
