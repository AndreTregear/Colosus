## 1. Executive Summary

Churn is Yaya's existential metric. At S/19-49/month targeting micro-businesses with <10 employees, Yaya faces the highest-churn segment in all of SaaS. Industry data shows micro-business SaaS churns at **8.9% monthly (69.1% annual)** — meaning without deliberate intervention, Yaya would lose two-thirds of its customers every year. But the data also reveals a clear playbook: companies with dedicated SMB success teams narrow the churn gap from 6.4% to 3.5%. Onboarding quality is the single highest-leverage intervention, as 43% of all SMB losses occur in the first 90 days. And usage-based pricing reduces churn by 46% compared to flat-rate models. This document maps every churn benchmark to Yaya's specific situation and designs a retention strategy purpose-built for informal micro-businesses in Peru.

## 3. Critical Churn Dynamics for Yaya

### 3.1 The First 90 Days Are Everything

**43% of all SMB customer losses occur within the first quarter post-purchase.** This means nearly half of all churn is an onboarding problem, not a product problem. For Yaya:

- **Onboarding must happen entirely within WhatsApp** — no separate app, no website, no complexity
- **Time-to-value must be under 5 minutes** — the customer sends their first voice note, Yaya logs it, and they see their first "daily sales summary" within the first session
- **The "aha moment" must be immediate** — for beauty salons, that's the first automated appointment reminder that prevents a no-show; for retail, it's the first daily sales total they've never had before

### 3.2 Product Usage Predicts Churn 90 Days in Advance

**Product usage declines by an average of 41% in the quarter preceding cancellation.** This gives Yaya a clear early warning system:

- **Track daily WhatsApp interactions per user** — any user who drops from 5+ messages/day to <1/day is at risk
- **Trigger automated re-engagement** when usage drops: "Hola María! No hemos hablado en 3 días. ¿Cómo van las ventas esta semana?"
- **Weekly analytics summaries** create checking habits even when the user isn't actively logging transactions

### 3.3 Buyer Seniority Matters — Yaya's Advantage

**Software purchased by C-suite executives churns 3.6× slower than tools purchased by individual contributors.** In micro-businesses, the owner IS the C-suite. Yaya's user is the business owner — the person with the most at stake. This structural advantage should push Yaya's churn below the micro-business average.

### 3.4 The SMB Success Team Effect

**The SMB-to-mid-market churn gap (6.4% vs 2.8%) narrows to just 1.1% when companies deploy dedicated SMB customer success teams.** For Yaya, this means:

- WhatsApp-based "customer success" automation can replicate much of what a human CS team does
- Weekly check-ins, proactive tips, and usage-based suggestions delivered as WhatsApp messages
- Community WhatsApp groups where users help each other (peer-to-peer success)

### 3.5 Involuntary Churn — The Free Win

**Involuntary churn (payment failures) represents $1.3 billion in recoverable SaaS revenue annually. Expired credit cards account for 42% of all payment failures.**

Yaya's Yape/Plin weekly billing model eliminates most involuntary churn by design:
- No credit cards = no expired card failures
- Weekly billing at S/5 = smaller amounts, less payment friction
- Yape/Plin payments are mobile-native, real-time, and habitual
- Smart retry + grace period for missed weekly payments

### 3.6 Vertical SaaS Premium

**Vertical SaaS products achieve retention 20-30% better than horizontal products at similar price points.** Yaya's vertical-specific features (beauty salon scheduling, construction quoting, restaurant ordering) create specialized workflows that general-purpose tools can't match — creating natural switching costs.

## 5. The Yaya Retention Playbook

### 5.1 Structural Retention (Built Into the Product)

**5.1.1 Transaction Data Lock-In**
Every sale logged, every invoice sent, every customer contacted through Yaya creates a data asset that becomes more valuable over time. After 6 months, a user has:
- Complete sales history and trends
- Customer contact database with purchase history
- Inventory records and supplier patterns
- Tax compliance records for SUNAT

Leaving Yaya means losing all of this — or manually exporting and recreating it elsewhere. This is ERP-level switching cost achieved organically.

**5.1.2 Customer-Facing Value**
When Yaya sends appointment reminders to a salon's customers, those customers save Yaya's number and expect reminders. Canceling Yaya means:
- Customers stop receiving reminders → no-shows increase
- Payment links stop working → collection friction increases
- The professional appearance disappears

This creates dual-sided switching costs — the business owner AND their customers would be disrupted.

**5.1.3 Usage-Based Revenue Component**
The 1.5% transaction fee creates natural alignment: customers pay more only when they process more business. This usage-based component alone should reduce churn by up to 46% compared to flat-rate pricing (per Focus Digital data).

**5.1.4 SUNAT Compliance Dependency**
Once a business is sending SUNAT-compliant invoices through Yaya, switching means:
- Migrating tax records to a new system
- Learning new compliance workflows
- Risk of errors during transition
- Potential SUNAT audit exposure

This creates regulatory switching costs that are nearly impossible to overcome.

### 5.2 Onboarding Retention (The Critical First 90 Days)

**5.2.1 Five-Minute Value Delivery**
The onboarding flow must deliver tangible value within the first WhatsApp conversation:

1. **Minute 0-1:** "¡Hola! Soy Yaya, tu asistente de negocios. Cuéntame, ¿a qué te dedicas?" → Business type classification
2. **Minute 1-2:** "¡Genial! ¿Cuánto vendiste hoy?" → First sale logged via voice note or text
3. **Minute 2-3:** "Listo, registré tu primera venta. Mañana te mando tu resumen diario." → First value promise
4. **Minute 3-5:** Quick setup of 2-3 recurring features (reminders, inventory items, customer names)
5. **End of Day 1:** First daily summary delivered automatically → "Hoy vendiste S/320. ¡Buen día!"

**5.2.2 Progressive Feature Unlock**
Don't overwhelm users with features. Introduce them gradually:
- **Week 1:** Sales logging + daily summaries
- **Week 2:** Customer reminders + appointment scheduling
- **Week 3:** Inventory tracking
- **Week 4:** Invoicing (for users who need it)
- **Month 2:** Analytics and business insights
- **Month 3:** Payment collection + financial reports

**5.2.3 The "First Win" Framework**
Each vertical has a specific "first win" that creates an emotional anchor:

| Vertical | First Win | Timeline | Emotional Impact |
|----------|-----------|----------|-----------------|
| Beauty Salon | First prevented no-show via reminder | Day 2-5 | "Yaya just saved me S/80!" |
| Retail | First daily sales total they've never tracked | Day 1 | "I never knew exactly how much I sell" |
| Restaurant | First customer who finds them via WhatsApp | Week 1-2 | "New customer from Yaya!" |
| Construction | First automated quote sent | Day 3-7 | "That took me 2 hours before" |

### 5.3 Engagement Retention (Days 90+)

**5.3.1 Daily Habit Loops**
- Morning: "Buenos días, María. Ayer vendiste S/450. Tu meta semanal va en S/1,800 de S/2,500."
- Evening: "Tu resumen del día: 12 ventas, S/520, 3 citas confirmadas para mañana."
- Weekly: "📊 Esta semana vendiste 15% más que la anterior. ¡Tu mejor semana del mes!"

**5.3.2 Milestone Celebrations**
- "¡100 ventas registradas! Ya tienes más datos que el 95% de negocios en tu zona."
- "6 meses con Yaya. Has registrado S/180,000 en ventas. ¿Quieres ver tu reporte semestral?"
- "Tu cliente más frecuente es Ana García (15 visitas). ¿Quieres mandarle un descuento?"

**5.3.3 Social Proof and Community**
- WhatsApp group for Yaya users in the same vertical
- Anonymous benchmarking: "Tu negocio vende 20% más que el promedio de salones en tu distrito"
- Referral program: "Invita a otro negocio y ambos obtienen un mes gratis"

### 5.4 Win-Back Retention (Churn Prevention)

**5.4.1 Churn Prediction Triggers**
Monitor for:
- Usage drop >40% week-over-week
- No messages sent in 3+ consecutive days
- Failed payment (even weekly Yape)
- Negative sentiment in messages

**5.4.2 Re-Engagement Sequence**
1. **Day 3 of inactivity:** Casual check-in: "¡Hola! ¿Todo bien con el negocio? Estoy aquí si necesitas algo."
2. **Day 7:** Value reminder: "Tip: Los negocios que usan Yaya diario ahorran en promedio 5 horas por semana."
3. **Day 14:** Personal outreach from founder (for first 500 users)
4. **Day 21:** "Si necesitas una pausa, puedo congelar tu cuenta gratis por 30 días." (Pause option reduces churn by 12-15% per SerpSculpt data)
5. **Day 30:** Exit survey + "¿Qué podemos mejorar?"

**5.4.3 Smart Cancellation Flow**
Exit surveys combined with smart cancellation offers cut voluntary churn by 12-15%:
- Offer a free month if reason is "too expensive"
- Offer simplified features if reason is "too complex"
- Offer pause if reason is "not using it right now"
- Ask for referral if reason is "closing my business" (they know other business owners)

For Yaya, reducing monthly churn from 8% (micro-business average) to 4% (target) doesn't just retain customers — it 2-3× the company's eventual valuation.
