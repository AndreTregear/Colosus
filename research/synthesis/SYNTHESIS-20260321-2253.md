## 2. New Research Summary

### 2.1 Meta WhatsApp AI Policy: Full Landscape (competitive/10)

**The headline:** Meta's ban on general-purpose AI chatbots from WhatsApp is **excellent news for Yaya.**

Key findings:
- **Yaya is explicitly compliant.** Meta's policy allows "structured, purpose-specific chatbots" for customer support, bookings, order tracking. Yaya is a task-specific business management tool — not a general-purpose AI assistant.
- **The ban is a competitive moat.** New entrants can't just wrap ChatGPT in WhatsApp. They must build genuinely task-specific functionality.
- **Competitors were disrupted.** Zapia (6M users) was forced to migrate off WhatsApp entirely. General-purpose wrappers are dead.
- **Vambe is unaffected** (task-specific) and raised $18.4M total, expanding to Mexico and Brazil.
- **April 2026 pricing update** has minimal Peru impact. Service messages (customer-initiated) remain free — and Yaya's primary flow is customer-initiated (user sends voice note).
- **Estimated WhatsApp API cost per Yaya user: $0.15-0.30/month** (mostly free service window + 2-3 utility templates/week)

**Strategic implication:** Meta has inadvertently created an environment where purpose-built business tools thrive and general-purpose AI wrappers die. This is exactly Yaya's lane.
