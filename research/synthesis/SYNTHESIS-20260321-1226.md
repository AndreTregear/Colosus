## 1. New Research Added This Cycle

### 1.1 BREAKING: Meta's WhatsApp AI Ban Crumbles Under Antitrust Pressure (competitive/06)

The most significant regulatory development since the research library began. Key findings:

- **Brazil's CADE forced Meta to reopen WhatsApp to third-party AI chatbots** (March 6, 2026). The EU followed within 24 hours under the Digital Markets Act
- **Meta now charges $0.0625 per non-template message** for third-party AI services in Brazil — expensive enough to deter high-volume general AI but establishing the precedent of platform openness
- **Zapia raised $19.3M** (from Prosus Ventures) despite being forced off WhatsApp. They migrated 6M users to a standalone app and are fighting Meta's policy across all LATAM jurisdictions
- **Yaya's task-specific classification remains safe** — Meta's ban targeted general-purpose AI assistants, not business bots. Invoicing, scheduling, inventory = explicitly allowed
- **The $0.0625 fee actually helps Yaya** — any competitor trying to offer AI business management on WhatsApp as a "general-purpose AI" faces per-message costs that Yaya doesn't. This is an unintentional cost moat
- **Peru is unaffected for now**, but Zapia has stated they'll challenge Meta's restrictions across all of LATAM. INDECOPI proceedings could follow within 12-18 months
- **Meta AI remains the #1 long-term threat** — if Meta builds native business management into its own AI on WhatsApp, no regulatory ruling helps. Yaya's moat must be vertical depth, not platform access

**Strategic implication:** The regulatory environment is more favorable for Yaya than previously assessed. Even if Meta tightened policies in the future, CADE/EU precedent suggests regulators would intervene. Revised WhatsApp platform risk score: 22/25 (down from 25/25).

### 1.2 Vambe: $14M Series A, 1,700 Customers, Brazil-Bound (competitive/07)

The most detailed competitive analysis of Yaya's closest funded comparator:

- **$14M Series A** led by Monashees (top-tier LATAM VC), December 2025. **Simón Borrero (Rappi's CEO)** joined the board
- **1,700+ active implementations**, processing millions of monthly conversations
- **17% MoM growth** since early 2025, team expanded from 22 to 80+
- **Entering Brazil H1 2026** — LATAM's largest market with 150M+ WhatsApp Business users
- **Building "Agent-to-Agent Recommendation Ad Engine"** — AI agents from different companies collaborating for cross-recommendations. This is a network-effects play

**Critical competitive assessment:**
- Vambe serves **mid-to-large B2C companies** (automotive dealerships, universities, fintechs). They require consulting, change management, and implementation teams per customer
- Yaya serves **micro-businesses** (beauty salons, street vendors, restaurants). Self-service via WhatsApp, $13/month
- **These are NOT the same market.** A beauty salon owner earning $800/month will never be a Vambe customer. An automotive dealership group will never use Yaya
- Vambe **validates Yaya's thesis** without competing: WhatsApp-native AI commerce works in LATAM, investors fund it, and demand is real
- Vambe has **no ERP depth** (no inventory, no invoicing, no tax compliance), **no compliance angle** (no SUNAT), and **cloud-dependent costs** ($0.01-0.05/query vs Yaya's $0.001)

**For investor pitches:** *"Vambe raised $14M to serve the top 1% of LATAM businesses with AI sales agents. We're building for the other 99% — the 40M micro-businesses that need operational management, not sales automation."*
