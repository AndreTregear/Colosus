## 2. New Research Summary

### 2.1 Meta Business AI Threat Assessment (competitive/12)

**The headline:** Meta is spending $60-65B on AI in 2026, but 97%+ of its revenue comes from advertising. Meta Business AI handles customer-facing interactions (FAQ, product recommendations, sales). It does NOT do business operations (inventory, invoicing, accounting, tax compliance). Meta and Yaya are complementary, not competitive.

Key findings:
- **Meta Q4 2025:** $59.89B revenue (+24% YoY), $200.97B full-year, 3.58B daily active users
- **Meta Business AI capabilities:** Automated FAQ, personalized product recommendations, sales assistance, after-hours support — all customer-facing
- **What Meta Business AI does NOT do:** Inventory management, financial record-keeping, invoicing, tax compliance, appointment scheduling, voice note processing, business analytics
- **The chatbot ban:** Meta banned general-purpose AI assistants from WhatsApp API in January 2026, but explicitly exempts "businesses using AI to serve customers" — Yaya falls under the exemption
- **EU forced 12-month openness** (March 2026) but at punitive per-message pricing (€0.049–€0.132)
- **Five threat scenarios assessed:** Meta building ERP (10% probability), Meta AI cannibalizing Yaya's AI layer (25%), Meta restricting API access (20%), Meta acquiring a LATAM competitor (15%), Meta enabling third-party AI agents officially (30% — the best scenario for Yaya)
- **The coexistence model:** Meta handles the "front office" (customer interactions), Yaya handles the "back office" (business operations) — similar to Shopify/Facebook Shops coexistence

**Strategic implication:** Meta is not the enemy. Meta is the distribution layer. Yaya should position as a business management tool, not an AI assistant, and maintain strict API compliance. The channel-agnostic architecture remains essential insurance.

### 2.2 African WhatsApp Commerce Lessons (market/17)

**The headline:** Africa has the world's most advanced WhatsApp commerce ecosystem — 5+ years ahead of LATAM. Every pain point African SMEs face is exactly what Yaya solves. But NO African startup has built voice-first business management, full ERP in WhatsApp, or tax compliance integration. Yaya is building what the world's most mature WhatsApp market hasn't figured out yet.

Key findings:
- **M-Pesa + WhatsApp integration:** Kenya processes KES 36 trillion ($275B+) annually through mobile money, often coordinated via WhatsApp. The Yape/Plin equivalent is less mature but growing fast.
- **Chpter (Kenya, $1.2M pre-seed):** The closest African analog to Yaya — WhatsApp commerce OS with M-Pesa integration. But Chpter is B2C sales automation, not business operations management. No voice, no ERP, no tax compliance.
- **The "digital ceiling":** African businesses hit the same ceiling Peruvian businesses will: unstructured data, no analytics, payment reconciliation nightmares, regulatory invisibility.
- **2026 = AI agents, not chatbots:** Kenya's tech community is emphatic — LLM-powered agents that reason, call tools, and process payments are the new standard. Rule-based bots are "table stakes."
- **Women are primary users:** 70% of UNDP "Innovate Your Market" participants were women; 51% of Peruvian micro-enterprises are women-owned.
- **Layer-2 thesis is investable:** African investors (Techstars, Norrsken, Cellulant co-founder) are funding WhatsApp commerce infrastructure. The thesis translates to LATAM.
- **Platform risk is real and acknowledged:** The October 2021 outage disrupted commerce across Africa. Channel-agnostic architecture is not paranoia — it's industry standard practice.

**Strategic implication:** Yaya has global relevance. The voice-first Conversational ERP model doesn't exist anywhere — not in Africa, not in India, not in Southeast Asia. If Yaya succeeds in Peru, it has expansion potential to every WhatsApp-dominant emerging market.

### 2.3 Peru Beyond Lima: Regional Expansion (market/18)

**The headline:** Peru's 82% internet penetration and 83.9% 4G coverage mean major cities beyond Lima are ready for Yaya. Arequipa, Trujillo, and Cusco should be Phase 2 targets. SUNAT's enforcement wave creates rolling geographic demand.

Key findings:
- **16 of 25 departments positive in Q2 2025:** Apurímac (+27.3%), Tacna (+13.8%), Junín (+11.8%), Piura (+6.0%) all outpacing Lima (+2.8%)
- **Tier 1 cities (Phase 2):** Arequipa (★★★★★), Trujillo (★★★★☆), Cusco (★★★★☆)
- **Tier 2 cities (Phase 3):** Piura, Chiclayo, Huancayo
- **UNDP precedent:** "Innovate Your Market" digitalized 2,000+ market entrepreneurs across 7 regions with 72% increase in digital trust
- **CMAC partnership opportunity:** Regional savings banks (Caja Arequipa, Caja Trujillo) are the financial infrastructure AND distribution channel outside Lima
- **S/29 "Lite" tier becomes essential** for markets with lower average incomes
- **Revenue model:** Regional expansion is 2-3× market multiplier, but with declining ARPU compensated by embedded finance data value
- **Voice AI consideration:** Regional accent diversity (Arequipa, Cusco) requires training data collection early in Phase 2

**Strategic implication:** Lima is the beachhead, not the ceiling. Regional expansion follows SUNAT's enforcement wave and existing beauty salon networks. Yaya should plan regional voice model fine-tuning as a Phase 2 priority.
