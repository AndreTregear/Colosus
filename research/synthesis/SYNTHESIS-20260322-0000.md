## 2. New Research Summary

### 2.1 Habit Formation & Behavioral Design (strategy/21)

**The headline:** Yaya has the most favorable behavioral design position of any SaaS product in LATAM, because it operates inside an existing deeply-ingrained habit (WhatsApp) and requires zero new behavior learning (voice notes).

Key findings:
- **Hook Model mapping:** Yaya naturally delivers all four phases — trigger (money uncertainty, 3–5× daily), action (voice note, zero learning cost), variable reward (daily insights that change), investment (irreplaceable transaction history)
- **7-day activation window:** If a user completes 3+ core loops in week 1, predicted retention at day 30 jumps from ~35% to ~73% (Amplitude benchmark)
- **Churn math:** Reducing monthly churn from 5% to 2% triples LTV (from $104 to $195). This is the single highest-leverage metric for the business.
- **Loss aversion:** After 3 months of data accumulation, canceling Yaya means losing 100+ transactions, 30+ client records, and automated reminders that clients now expect. Switching cost is behavioral, not financial.
- **Network effect lock-in:** When Yaya manages client reminders, the clients themselves develop habits around receiving confirmations — canceling Yaya disrupts 30+ people's habits simultaneously.
- **Anti-churn architecture:** Streak mechanism, business health score, proactive loss-aversion messaging, and network effect lock-in create a multi-layered defense.

**Strategic implication:** Don't build features; build habits. The #1 priority for MVP isn't more functionality — it's engineering the daily habit loop.

### 2.2 Peru Business Software Pricing Landscape (competitive/11)

**The headline:** Yaya doesn't compete against Alegra. It competes against paper notebooks. 75% of the target market uses zero business software.

Key findings:
- **Verified pricing (March 2026):** Alegra Emprendedor S/49, PYME S/89, Pro S/149. Defontana starts at S/299+. Bsale S/99–299. CONCAR S/150/year.
- **The gap:** All existing software requires computer literacy, accounting vocabulary, and form-based data entry. None serves the 3.5M informal micro-enterprises that manage via WhatsApp.
- **Alegra's "WhatsApp" feature is superficial:** It sends invoice PDFs via WhatsApp. It does NOT enable business management through conversation.
- **No competitor uses AI meaningfully:** "Reportes inteligentes" in existing products means pre-formatted charts, not AI-generated insights.
- **The contador channel is wide open:** No competitor offers economically-incentivized referral programs for accountants.
- **S/29 tier recommendation:** At 3.4% of monthly income (vs. S/49 = 5.7%), a "Lite" tier could dramatically expand the conversion funnel for price-sensitive necessity entrepreneurs.

**Strategic implication:** Yaya's pricing is validated by Alegra's S/49 anchor, but its real advantage is 10× lower cognitive cost. Same price, voice-first, zero computer required.

### 2.3 Conversational ERP Category Definition (technology/17)

**The headline:** Yaya is creating a new software category — Conversational ERP — where the interface is conversation and the AI adapts to the human, not the other way around. This category didn't exist before 2025 because the technology prerequisites weren't met.

Key findings:
- **Four eras of business software:** Ledger digitization (1970s) → Graphical ERP (1990s) → Cloud SaaS (2010s) → Conversational ERP (2025+). Yaya is the first entrant in Era 4.
- **Why now:** LLM inference cost collapse (95%+), Whisper v3 accuracy for LATAM Spanish, WhatsApp Cloud API stability — all converged in 2025–2026.
- **The "No-UI UI":** The most powerful interface is invisible. Rosa doesn't feel like she's "using software." She feels like she's talking to an assistant.
- **Incumbents can't pivot (Innovator's Dilemma):** Alegra adding a chatbot doesn't make them Conversational ERP. Their architecture, culture, business model, and customer base all prevent genuine pivoting.
- **The Conversational CEO daily time:** 3 minutes of voice notes vs. 30–60 minutes of screen time with traditional ERP. 10× efficiency improvement.
- **Voice captures richer data than forms:** A single 10-second voice note ("Vendí 3 cortes a María y su hija, pagaron juntas con Yape, María vuelve en 3 semanas para tinte") contains quantitative, relational, contextual, and predictive information that no form could capture.

**Strategic implication:** The investor pitch is: "ERP has always forced humans to think like computers. We built the first ERP that thinks like a human. That's why it works for the 40M businesses that rejected everything before it."

## 7. Top Insights for Andre (Cycle #18)

### 1. Build Habits, Not Features
The #1 priority for MVP isn't more functionality. It's engineering the daily habit loop: Rosa records a voice note → Yaya confirms → Evening summary → Rosa checks. If this loop runs 3+ times in week 1, retention probability jumps from 35% to 73%. Every engineering hour should be measured against: "Does this strengthen the daily loop?"

### 2. You're Competing Against Paper, Not Alegra
Stop worrying about Alegra's feature set. 75% of your market uses NOTHING. Your competition is the paper notebook and human memory. The question isn't "Is Yaya better than Alegra?" — it's "Is Yaya easier than doing nothing?" And the answer is yes, because Yaya adds value to what Rosa is already doing (sending voice notes).

### 3. You're Creating a Category — Own It
"Conversational ERP" is the category. Frame every conversation — with investors, press, customers — around this: "ERP forced humans to think like computers. We built the first ERP that thinks like a human." This isn't marketing fluff; it's the literal product architecture.

### 4. Consider the S/29 "Lite" Tier

### 5. The Voice Note Is Richer Than Any Form
A single 10-second voice note contains more information — quantitative, relational, contextual, and predictive — than any form-based ERP could capture in 3 minutes of typing. This isn't just a UX improvement; it's a data advantage. Yaya will have richer business data per user than any competitor, which powers better insights, better credit scoring, and better embedded finance.
