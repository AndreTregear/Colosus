### 1.3 Conversational CEO Product Specification (technology/07)

The product vision translated into concrete UX flows, technical requirements, and implementation roadmap. Key findings:

- **Complete user persona** (Rosa Huamán — 34-year-old salon owner in San Borja) with daily workflow mapped
- **5 core feature flows** fully specified: appointment scheduling, sales/revenue tracking, invoice generation (SUNAT/Nubefact), inventory management, payment collection
- **Voice-first design principles:** Voice notes as primary input, text responses as primary output, proactive notifications, conversation as interface (no menus)
- **Onboarding flow: 5 minutes to first value** — minute-by-minute conversation design
- **Progressive disclosure model:** Week 1 (appointments), Week 2–3 (insights), Month 2 (inventory/payments), Month 3 (invoicing), Month 4+ (advanced analytics)
- **Technical requirements:** Whisper Large V3 fine-tuned for Peruvian Spanish; <2 sec STT latency; >95% intent recognition; zero hallucination for financial data (deterministic validation layer)
- **Cultural design patterns:** Informal "tú", Peruvian expressions, emoji-native, trust-building through consistency and transparency
- **Multimodal strategy:** Voice input + text/visual output + interactive buttons for quick decisions
- **Product roadmap in 6 phases:** "La Agenda" → "La Caja" → "La Factura" → "El Cobro" → "El Asesor" → "El Banco"

**Strategic implication:** The Conversational CEO is now a concrete, buildable product — not just a vision document. The UX flows can be directly implemented. The technical requirements are achievable with existing infrastructure (c.yaya.sh + Whisper + local LLM).
