## 2. New Research Added This Cycle

### 2.1 LATAM SaaS Churn & Retention (strategy/03)

**Key findings:**
- **SMB SaaS faces 3–7% monthly churn** (31–58% annual) — Yaya's target segment has the highest attrition in all of SaaS
- **LATAM SMB NRR is ~96%** (Riverwood Capital survey of 150 companies) — the average customer base shrinks 4%/year before new logos
- **43% of all SMB losses occur in the first 90 days** — onboarding is everything
- **At $10–25/month ARPU, Yaya sits in the highest-churn bracket** (~6.1% monthly for <$25 ARPU)
- **Vertical SaaS in LATAM achieves NRR >120%** — significantly outperforming horizontal peers
- **WhatsApp provides a structural retention advantage:** 98% open rates for re-engagement vs. 22% for email
- **Auto-pause > auto-cancel** in cash-flow-volatile informal economies — can save 15–30% of would-be churners
- **Transaction history as conversation thread** creates the equivalent of enterprise "integration switching costs" through natural use
- **Unit economics model:** At 5% monthly churn and $15 ARPU, LTV = $240 on $30 CAC = 8x LTV:CAC — excellent
- **Embedded finance drops churn to 1–2%** — financial services SaaS has the lowest churn of any vertical

### 2.2 AI Accuracy for Financial Compliance (technology/03)

**Key findings:**
- **General LLMs hallucinate 46–79% of the time** on factual queries (Vectara 2025, Nature 2025)
- **50% of UK firms have suffered financial losses** from clients relying on chatbot tax/financial advice (Dext, Jan 2026)
- **No local LLM passes CPA exams** — best performer (granite3.3:8b) scores 58.7%, still failing 4/10 questions
- **Tax compliance accuracy: 38.7–48.0%** for tested models — essentially coin-flip quality
- **The "No LLM Zone" principle:** Critical financial calculations (IGV, invoice totals, SUNAT compliance) must be **deterministic code, never LLM-generated**
- **Confirmation flows are mandatory:** Every financial transaction must show itemized details, get explicit user confirmation before recording
- **Voice-to-data accuracy is ~75–80% end-to-end** — insufficient for financial records without confirmation step
- **Peru's Law 31814 requires:** AI transparency disclosure, human escalation paths, and audit trails for high-risk applications
- **Credit scoring = "high-risk AI"** under Law 31814 — triggers enhanced oversight, bias monitoring, and documentation requirements
- **The architecture must be hybrid:** LLM for natural language understanding + deterministic code for calculations + RAG for data retrieval

### 2.3 WhatsApp API Pricing Economics (strategy/04)

**Key findings:**
- **Service messages (user-initiated) are completely free** and unlimited — this is Yaya's primary message type
- **Peru utility messages: $0.02/msg** — daily summaries, reminders, confirmations
- **Peru marketing messages: $0.0703/msg** — 3.5x more expensive than utility
- **Yaya's monthly Meta cost per user: $0.60–$1.20** (vs. $25–$35 for marketing-focused competitors)
- **This represents a 30x messaging cost advantage** over marketing-focused WhatsApp platforms
- **At $15 ARPU, WhatsApp costs = 4–8% of revenue** — enabling gross margins of 87–94%
- **Volume tier discounts:** At 100K users, per-message utility costs drop 25% automatically
- **Colombia is the cheapest LATAM market:** Utility at $0.0008/msg — essentially free
- **Click-to-WhatsApp ads provide 72-hour free messaging** — zero-cost acquisition channel
- **Meta's January 2026 AI ban** requires Yaya to position as specialized business tool, not general AI assistant
- **BSP strategy:** Start with zero-markup BSP; transition to direct API at 5,000+ users

## 4. Cross-Document Insights (Connections Across All 18 Documents)

### 4.1 The "Free Service Messages" × "Transaction History Moat" Convergence
**Connection:** strategy/04 (WhatsApp pricing) + strategy/03 (retention) + competitive/02 (India lessons)

The most powerful insight from the new research: **the more the user talks to Yaya, the more data accumulates (retention moat), and it costs Yaya nothing (free service messages)**. This creates a unique economic dynamic where the retention mechanism (conversation history) is essentially free to operate. In traditional SaaS, deeper engagement = higher infrastructure costs. In Yaya's model, deeper engagement = free messages + stronger lock-in. This is the WhatsApp-native equivalent of a zero-marginal-cost network effect.

### 4.2 The Accuracy-Trust-Retention Triangle
**Connection:** technology/03 (AI accuracy) + market/03 (trust) + strategy/03 (retention)

Peru's trust deficit (lowest in LATAM) intersects with AI accuracy requirements in a specific way: **a single financial error by Yaya could destroy months of trust accumulation**. In a low-trust culture where 72% of accountants report clients challenging professional advice with "the AI said X," Yaya's confirmation flows serve triple duty:
1. **Accuracy function:** Catches LLM errors before they become records
2. **Trust function:** Shows the user that Yaya respects their knowledge ("¿Correcto?")
3. **Retention function:** Each confirmed transaction is another data point that makes leaving costly

### 4.3 The "Vertical SaaS + Embedded Finance" Retention Pathway
**Connection:** strategy/03 (churn) + fintech/02 (lending) + strategy/02 (investment landscape)

The retention research reveals that vertical SaaS achieves NRR >120% in LATAM while horizontal achieves ~96%. The fintech research shows Peru's $6–8B credit gap and 38–46% interest rates for microenterprises. The synthesis: **Yaya should launch as vertical SaaS (restaurants, retail, beauty) and evolve into embedded finance**. This two-stage approach:
- Stage 1: Vertical focus → NRR >120% (retention via depth)
- Stage 2: Credit scoring from transaction data → financial services churn dynamics (1–2% monthly)
- Result: From 5% monthly churn (SMB SaaS average) to <2% (financial infrastructure)

### 4.4 The "Pricing Moat" — Why Competitors Can't Match Yaya's Price
**Connection:** strategy/04 (WhatsApp pricing) + technology/01 (LLM economics) + competitive/01 (landscape)

Yaya's cost structure creates a pricing moat that cloud-dependent, marketing-focused competitors cannot replicate:
- **Local LLM:** $0.11–$0.15/user/month (vs. $5–15/user/month for cloud API)
- **Utility messaging:** $0.60–$1.20/user/month (vs. $25–$35 for marketing messages)
- **Total COGS:** $0.91–$1.95/user/month
- **Gross margin:** 87–94%

This enables Yaya to charge **$10/month** and still maintain ~80% gross margins. No cloud-dependent competitor can match this price while covering their API and messaging costs. The moat is structural, not strategic — it's embedded in the architecture decisions.

## 6. Top 10 Insights for Andre (Updated)

1. **WhatsApp's pricing structure is Yaya's secret weapon.** Service messages are free. Utility messages cost $0.02 in Peru. Marketing competitors pay $0.07. At scale, Yaya's messaging costs are 30x lower than competitors'. This enables a $10/month price point that no one can match.

2. **Expect to lose half your customers in Year 1 — and that's normal.** SMB SaaS in LATAM has ~96% NRR. Early-stage companies see 55% annual churn. The goal isn't zero churn; it's quarter-over-quarter improvement.

3. **43% of churn happens in the first 90 days.** Onboarding isn't important — it's everything. Yaya's "Aha! Moment" must happen in <5 minutes: user sends a voice note about a sale → Yaya instantly records it and shows a running total.

4. **Never let the AI do math.** LLMs hallucinate on financial data 46–79% of the time. All calculations (totals, IGV, invoice amounts) must be deterministic code. The LLM's job is to understand what the user said, not to compute anything.

5. **Transaction history is the moat that builds itself for free.** Every conversation message costs $0.00 (service message). Every recorded transaction makes it harder to leave. After 90 days, the WhatsApp thread is the most complete record of the business — and it cost Yaya nothing to create.

6. **India validates the thesis at $4.2B scale.** No Indian player has combined AI intelligence + full ERP + WhatsApp-native. LATAM is 3–4 years behind. The window is open.

7. **Go vertical first, then expand.** LATAM vertical SaaS achieves NRR >120% while horizontal achieves ~96%. Start with restaurants (highest WhatsApp commerce adoption), then retail, then beauty. Go deep in 2–3 verticals before going broad.

8. **Embedded finance is the endgame that changes everything.** Moving from "SaaS tool" to "financial infrastructure" drops churn from 5% monthly to 1–2%. Peru's $6–8B credit gap and 38–46% microenterprise interest rates make this enormously valuable for users.

9. **87–94% gross margins are achievable from day one.** Local LLM ($0.15/user) + utility messaging ($1.20/user) + server ($0.50/user) = $1.85 total COGS on $15 ARPU. This is venture-fundable unit economics.

10. **Peru's new AI law (Law 31814) requires transparency and audit trails.** Every AI interaction must disclose that it's AI, offer human escalation, and maintain traceable records. Build this from day one — it's both legally required and builds trust in Peru's low-trust culture.
