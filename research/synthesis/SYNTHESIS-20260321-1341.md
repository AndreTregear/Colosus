## 1. New Research Added This Cycle

### 1.1 Peru's AI Law: Yaya's Regulatory Asset (risks/04)

Peru is one of only two LATAM countries with both an enacted AI law (Ley 31814) AND implementing regulations (DS 115-2025-PCM, September 2025). Key findings:

- **Risk classification system**: Yaya's core features (scheduling, invoicing, inventory) fall under **low risk** with minimal compliance overhead. Future fintech features (credit scoring) require **high-risk** compliance — which is itself a moat
- **MIPYME support provisions**: The law explicitly creates regulatory sandboxes, innovation laboratories, and MIPYME-specific support measures coordinated with PRODUCE (Ministry of Production)
- **Regulatory sandbox opportunity**: Yaya should apply to participate in Peru's AI regulatory sandbox — it's a massive credibility signal for both customers and investors
- **Brazil comparison**: Brazil's AI bill (2,338/2023) is still in the Chamber of Deputies. Peru's head start means Yaya's compliance experience transfers directly when expanding to Brazil
- **Private sector obligations**: Companies must maintain records of AI system operations, implement privacy protocols, and ensure human supervision for high-risk systems. For Yaya's current MVP, this means basic documentation — not a technical rebuild

**Strategic implication:** Peru's AI law is an **asset, not a liability**. It creates regulatory moats, provides government support pathways, and positions Yaya as a responsible AI provider. Add to investor pitch: *"We operate within Latin America's most comprehensive AI regulatory framework."*

### 1.2 WhatsApp Per-Message Pricing: 68% Cost Reduction (strategy/13)

Meta's July 2025 shift from conversation-based to per-message pricing fundamentally improves Yaya's unit economics:

- **All customer-initiated interactions are free**: When the user messages the bot, all AI responses are free "service" messages. No cap, no limit
- **Utility messages free within service window**: Invoice generation, appointment confirmations, inventory alerts — all free if sent during an active conversation
- **Peru-specific rates**: Marketing $0.0703/msg (one of LATAM's highest), Utility $0.0200/msg, Authentication $0.0377/msg. Yaya avoids the expensive categories
- **Revised cost per user**: WhatsApp messaging dropped from **$1.20/user/month to $0.38/user/month** — a 68% reduction
- **Gross margin improvement**: From 84.2% to **90.5%** at $13/month subscription
- **Breakeven drops to ~11 users** (from ~12)
- **Cost optimization strategies**: Batch daily reminders into single messages, use Click-to-WhatsApp Ads (72-hour free window) for acquisition, maximize in-window utility messages
- **BSP recommendation**: Start with Meta Cloud API directly (no markup, no monthly fees) or 360dialog

**Competitive impact**: Traditional CRM chatbots ($2.50-5.00/user/month in messaging costs) and marketing platforms ($5.00-15.00) face 3-10x higher messaging costs than Yaya's customer-initiated model.

### 1.3 SUNAT SIRE: The Killer Feature (technology/10)

Peru's SIRE (Sistema Integrado de Registros Electrónicos) became mandatory for principal taxpayers in January 2026 and is expanding. Key findings:

- **SIRE is genuinely painful**: Monthly electronic sales and purchase register submission, cross-referencing with SUNAT proposals, 3-day CPE submission deadlines, fines of S/ 1,500-15,000 for non-compliance
- **The accountant displacement play**: Micro-businesses currently pay S/ 200-500/month for accountants to handle SUNAT compliance. Yaya at $13/month is a 15-40x cost reduction
- **February 2026 IGV rate change**: SUNAT updated validation rules (IGV from 10% to 10.5%) — demonstrating why automated, up-to-date compliance systems are valuable
- **Electronic factoring bridge**: Invoices generated through Yaya can become negotiable instruments for factoring, enabling liquidity access for micro-businesses. Factoring referral commissions (0.5-1% of invoice value) could exceed subscription revenue
- **Formalization funnel**: 86% of MYPEs are informal. As SUNAT tightens enforcement, Yaya can be "the reason I went formal" — turning regulatory pressure into a customer acquisition engine
- **Technical moat**: Building full SUNAT compliance (UBL 2.1, OSE integration, SIRE API, digital certificates, 5-year retention) is a 12-18 month investment that competitors can't shortcut

**Market sizing update**: Total addressable market expands to ~1.7M businesses when including informal MYPEs considering formalization.
