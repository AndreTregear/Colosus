## 2. New Research Summary

### 2.1 Peru Data Protection & AI Compliance (risks/07)

**The headline:** Voice notes are likely classifiable as biometric/sensitive data under Peru's updated PDPL (effective March 30, 2025). This sounds scary but is manageable with one key architectural decision: self-host Whisper and delete audio immediately after transcription.

Key findings:
- **New PDPL regulation** (Supreme Decree 016-2024-JUS) expanded biometric data definition to include "data derived from biometric data which by itself renders a data subject identifiable" — voice recordings almost certainly qualify
- **Written consent required** for sensitive data processing (not just click-through)
- **48-hour breach notification** now mandatory to ANPD
- **DPO requirement phased:** Micro-enterprises (<150 UIT / ~$217K revenue) have until November 30, 2028
- **AI Law 31814 + SD 115-2025-PCM:** Risk-based classification. Yaya's invoicing/financial components = high-risk. But micro-enterprise grace period extends to September 2028+
- **ANPD enforcement increasing:** S/2.7M in fines in 2023, focus on digital services
- **Cross-border transfer risk:** Sending audio to cloud STT (OpenAI Whisper API) = cross-border biometric transfer requiring notification and safeguards
- **Self-hosted Whisper eliminates the hardest compliance issue** — no audio leaves Peru, no cross-border biometric transfer, dramatically simplified regulatory posture
- **SUNAT data has separate legal basis** — no consent needed for tax compliance processing, mandatory 5-year retention

**Strategic implication:** Compliance is achievable with architectural decisions made at design time. Self-host Whisper, delete audio immediately, collect written consent in onboarding, register database with ANPD. The grace periods give Yaya 2+ years of runway, but building compliance into the MVP is 10× cheaper than retrofitting.

### 2.2 Colombia Market Readiness (market/19)

**The headline:** Colombia is Yaya's natural second market — 50% larger than Peru, with MORE mature e-invoicing (mandatory since 2019), MORE advanced digital payments (21M+ Nequi users), and the SAME WhatsApp dominance (2.1M WhatsApp Business accounts, 42% YoY growth).

Key findings:
- **2.1 million WhatsApp Business accounts** (vs. Peru's 1.3M)
- **38 million WhatsApp users** (73% penetration)
- **DIAN e-invoicing mandatory since 2019** with aggressive enforcement (business closures in Medellín)
- **Nequi: 21M+ users** (Colombia's Yape equivalent, Bancolombia-owned)
- **Daviplata: 17M+ users** (Davivienda-owned)
- **74% of Colombian businesses** with digital presence actively sell via WhatsApp
- **57% of micro-enterprises are women-owned** (same as Peru)
- **Bre-B interoperable payments** launched 2024 (Colombia's emerging Pix equivalent)
- **No competitor in Colombia** combines WhatsApp-native + voice-first + DIAN e-invoicing
- **Medellín is the natural beachhead** (startup culture, beauty sector density, tech-savvy population)
- **Peru's stricter compliance automatically exceeds Colombia's current requirements** — architectural advantage of starting in the harder market

**Strategic implication:** Plan for Colombia from day one. The technical adaptation is modest (dialect, DIAN API, Nequi integration). At 1% penetration of Colombian WhatsApp Business accounts = 21,000 users × ~$14/month = $296K USD/month. Execute Colombia in months 6–12 after Peru launch.

### 2.3 WhatsApp Voice AI Architecture (technology/18)

**The headline:** Building a voice-first system on WhatsApp is fundamentally an architecture problem, not a model problem. The reference design achieves sub-5-second end-to-end latency for common operations, costs ~$0.19/user/month to operate, and scales to 5,000 users on existing c.yaya.sh hardware.

Key findings:
- **WhatsApp is not a streaming audio channel** — voice notes are discrete media files processed asynchronously. This is the #1 misconception that causes projects to fail.
- **Complete pipeline:** Webhook → media download → self-hosted Whisper → NLU → business logic → response. Total latency budget: 3–5 seconds.
- **Three-tier NLU:** Rule-based regex for 70% of common patterns (<50ms) → local Qwen 27B for complex cases (200–800ms) → cloud LLM fallback for edge cases (1–3s)
- **Faster-whisper (CTranslate2):** 4× faster than standard Whisper, INT8 quantization for A5000
- **Cost per voice note:** ~$0.005 = 1.5% of S/49 monthly revenue per user
- **Scaling:** c.yaya.sh supports 2,000–5,000 DAU without additional hardware. At 5,000 users = $65K USD/month revenue, easily justifying next infrastructure investment.
- **The "acknowledge then complete" pattern:** Send instant text acknowledgment, then deliver full result 3–5 seconds later. Makes 8-second operations feel like 2-second operations.
- **Never lose a transaction:** Durable queuing, exactly-once processing, comprehensive audit trails. Voice notes are business records — losing one destroys trust.

**Strategic implication:** The engineering path from "Conversational CEO" concept to working prototype is now fully specified. The existing hardware supports the MVP and first 5,000 users. The 5-second latency target is achievable with self-hosted infrastructure.
