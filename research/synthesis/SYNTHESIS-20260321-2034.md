## 2. New Research Summary

### 2.1 WhatsApp Platform Dependency Risk (risks/05)

**The January 2026 Ban:** On October 15, 2025, Meta revised WhatsApp Business API terms to ban general-purpose AI assistants. By January 15, 2026, ChatGPT (50M users), Microsoft Copilot, Perplexity, and numerous startups were removed. EU opened antitrust investigation; Italy and Brazil issued interim suspensions — but enforcement proceeded.

**Yaya's vulnerability:** Currently safe (business tool, not general-purpose chatbot). Composite risk score: **55.5/100 (Medium-High)** across 5 scenarios including API restriction expansion (20% probability), pricing changes (35%), reliability degradation (25%), misclassification (12%), and regulatory changes (15%).

**Key mitigation:** Build a channel abstraction layer from Day 1 (~2-3 extra dev days, $0 cost). All business logic and data stored in PostgreSQL, never in WhatsApp conversations. Pluggable channel adapters allow adding SMS (Phase 2), Telegram (Phase 3), and PWA (Phase 4) without rebuilding core logic.

**Critical lesson:** "Build on WhatsApp, but never build ONLY on WhatsApp."

### 2.2 Scaling Unit Economics (strategy/19)

**Cost structure evolution:**

| Scale | Cost/User/Month | Gross Margin | Bottleneck |
|-------|----------------|-------------|------------|
| 1-50 users | $0.10 | 98.7% | Andre's time |
| 50-500 | $2.50-6.50 | 33-70% | Support costs (Andre) |
| 500-2,000 | $5-8 | 40-55% | GPU capacity approaching ceiling |
| 2,000-10,000 | $1.90-2.74 | 82% | WhatsApp API costs (50-65% of total) |

**Break-even:** ~300 paying users at S/29/month.  
**Path to $1M ARR:** 2-3 years via contador distribution channel.  
**Self-hosted AI advantage:** 10-20x cost advantage over competitors using cloud AI APIs.  
**Valley of death:** 100-500 users, where support costs spike but revenue doesn't yet justify hires.  
**Biggest cost at scale:** WhatsApp API messaging (not AI inference) — strongest argument for multi-channel.

### 2.3 Peru Salon Seasonal Revenue Patterns (market/15)

**December dependency:** Peruvian salons earn 140-180% of average monthly revenue in December. The rest of the year is "instabilidad" per APEB president Rebeca Antezana.

**Optimal launch timeline:**
- Build MVP: March-May 2026
- Customer Zero recruitment: June 2026 (post-Mother's Day, salon owners have bandwidth)
- Pilot: June-August 2026 (moderate traffic months)
- Expanded pilot: October-November 2026 (pre-December rush)
- **December stress test:** December 2026 (Yaya proves value during highest-revenue month)

**Marketing goldmine:** "¿Cuánto vendiste en diciembre? ¿Estás segura? Yaya sabe exactamente."

**Seasonal churn pattern:** Highest churn Jan-Feb (owners cut costs), lowest Oct-Nov (nobody cancels before peak month). Annual prepayment discount in Sep-Oct locks users through the high-churn window.

## 3. Cross-Document Insights (Cycle #15)

### 3.1 The WhatsApp Cost Paradox

A remarkable insight emerges from connecting risks/05 and strategy/19: WhatsApp is simultaneously Yaya's **greatest distribution advantage** AND its **largest cost at scale** AND its **biggest existential risk.**

| Dimension | WhatsApp's Role | Implication |
|-----------|----------------|-------------|
| Distribution | Only channel with 95%+ penetration in target market | Must be primary channel for user acquisition |
| Cost | 50-65% of total costs at 10,000 users | Must develop free alternatives (Telegram, PWA) |
| Risk | Meta can change rules unilaterally with 92 days' notice | Must build channel abstraction from Day 1 |

**Resolution:** WhatsApp is the **acquisition channel** (get users in). Free channels (Telegram, PWA) are the **retention channels** (keep users engaged at lower cost and lower risk). The channel abstraction layer serves triple duty: risk mitigation, cost optimization, and user resilience.

### 3.2 The Seasonal Data Moat

Connecting market/15 (seasonality) with strategy/16 (defensibility) reveals a powerful compound moat:

**Month 1-6:** Yaya collects baseline transaction data → basic reporting value  
**Month 7-12:** Yaya captures a complete seasonal cycle → year-over-year comparisons become possible  
**Year 2+:** Yaya has cross-salon seasonal patterns → predictive features no competitor can match  

A competitor entering the market 6 months after Yaya cannot offer "Here's how your December compares to last December" until they've been operating for 18 months. The seasonal data moat compounds with every cycle.

### 3.3 The Support Cost Cliff and Contador Solution

Strategy/19 identifies the "valley of death" at 100-500 users where support costs dominate. Market/13 (contador ecosystem) provides the solution: contadores serve as Yaya's outsourced support layer for their salon clients. The contador doesn't just distribute Yaya — they support it, reducing Andre's per-user support burden from 30 min/month to near-zero for contador-referred clients.

This means the critical metric isn't "users" but "contadores onboarded." Each contador brings 15-30 pre-supported users.

## 7. Top Insights for Andre (Cycle #15)

1. **Build the channel abstraction layer from Day 1.** This is the single most important architectural decision from this cycle's research. It takes 2-3 extra days during initial development and costs nothing, but it protects against the existential risk proven by Meta's January 2026 ban on third-party AI. Design your webhook handler with a clean interface: `receive_message(channel, user_id, content)` → business logic → `send_message(channel, user_id, response)`. WhatsApp is just one implementation of the channel interface.

2. **Your biggest cost at scale is WhatsApp, not AI.** Self-hosted Whisper and Qwen3.5-27B give you a 10-20x cost advantage over cloud-dependent competitors. But at 10,000 users, WhatsApp API messaging will be $12,000-15,000/month — more than all other costs combined. Plan for Telegram and PWA as cost-optimized alternatives for engaged users.

3. **Break-even is at ~300 paying users.** That's remarkably fast for SaaS. The contador channel can get you there: 15 contadores × 20 clients each = 300 businesses. Focus on signing contadores, not individual salon owners.

4. **The "valley of death" is 100-500 users.** Your time becomes the bottleneck. Every support ticket you handle personally costs more than the subscription revenue. The contador channel is the solution: contadores provide first-line support for their clients.

5. **Launch timing matters enormously.** Start building now, pilot in June, capture December data, launch publicly in January 2027. This gives you a complete seasonal cycle of data that no competitor can replicate. The January launch message: "¿Cuánto vendiste en diciembre? Yaya sabe exactamente."

6. **Register a Telegram bot today.** It takes 5 minutes, costs nothing, and gives you a fallback channel. Don't build the integration yet — just reserve the handle. Same for an SMS provider account.
