## 1. Executive Summary

The existing research library treats the Peru salon market as a steady-state opportunity. But salons are **deeply seasonal businesses**, and this seasonality has critical implications for when to launch Yaya, when to recruit Customer Zero, and how to design the product's value proposition. This document maps Peru's salon revenue calendar, identifies the emotional and financial rhythms of salon owners throughout the year, and recommends an optimal launch timing strategy. The core finding: **Yaya should launch its Customer Zero pilot in April-May 2026 to capture a complete revenue cycle through December**, and the "December dependency" problem — where salons earn 25-40% of annual profit in a single month — is itself a product opportunity.

## 2. The December Dependency Problem

### 2.1 Industry Data

According to Gestión (June 2025), the Peruvian salon industry exhibits an extreme seasonal pattern:

> "En el sector belleza en Perú, diciembre se vive como un salvavidas. Las agendas se llenan, los turnos se duplican y los equipos trabajan al tope. Pero esa 'buena racha' de fin de año contrasta con la inestabilidad de los otros 11 meses."
> — UEBEA digital agency analysis, December 2025

Translation: "In Peru's beauty sector, December is lived as a lifeline. Schedules fill up, shifts double, and teams work at capacity. But that 'good streak' at year-end contrasts with the instability of the other 11 months."

Rebeca Antezana, president of APEB (Asociación Peruana de Empresarios de la Belleza), confirmed to Gestión that **even the traditionally strong month of May (Mother's Day) hasn't recovered to pre-pandemic levels as of 2025.**

**Revenue index:** 100 = average monthly revenue. December routinely exceeds 140-180% of average.

### 2.3 The Emotional Calendar

This matters for Yaya because **salon owners' receptivity to new tools varies dramatically by season:**

**Best months to sell/onboard (receptivity high, time available):**
- **January-February:** Pain is fresh (December rush over, revenue dropped). Owners have time to try new things. They're thinking "I need to get organized." **But** — they're also cash-poor and may resist paying.
- **March-April:** Routine established, moderate workload. Good time for training. **But** — Easter week disrupts schedules.
- **August-September:** Quiet enough to adopt new tools, with enough lead time before December rush to see value. **Optimal onboarding window if you want December data.**

**Worst months to sell/onboard:**
- **November-December:** No time. Every minute is booked. No salon owner will learn a new system during their highest-revenue months.
- **May:** Mother's Day rush occupies the first two weeks; rest of month is recovery.

## 3. Implications for Yaya Launch Timing

### 3.1 The 12-Week MVP Plan (strategy/18) Mapped to Calendar

Current plan: 12 weeks of development → Customer Zero recruitment in Weeks 9-10.

If Andre starts building **now (late March 2026):**

| Week | Calendar | Activity |

| 1-2 (Mar 23 - Apr 5) | Late March → early April | Foundation: WhatsApp API, Whisper deployment |
| 3-4 (Apr 6 - Apr 19) | April (pre-Easter) | Core NLU, transaction recording |
| 5-6 (Apr 20 - May 3) | Late April → early May | WhatsApp Flows, confirmations |

| 9-10 (May 18 - May 31) | Late May | **Customer Zero recruitment** |

**Problem:** Recruiting a salon owner during **late May** (right after Mother's Day rush) is actually decent timing — they've just survived a busy period and are entering a quieter stretch. They have mental bandwidth to try something new.

**Opportunity:** If Yaya is live by June 2026, Customer Zero uses it through June → December. By December, Yaya has captured data from **the single most important revenue month** for the salon. This is critical for product validation and value demonstration.

### 3.2 Recommended Launch Calendar

| Phase | Dates | Why This Timing |
|-------|-------|-----------------|
| Build MVP | Mar 23 - May 31, 2026 | 10 weeks of focused development |
| Customer Zero Recruitment | Jun 1-15, 2026 | Post-Mother's Day, salon owners have time and fresh pain |
| Pilot (1-3 salons) | Jun 15 - Aug 31, 2026 | 2.5 months of daily usage through moderate-traffic months |
| Product iteration | Sep 1 - Oct 15, 2026 | Fix issues before the busy season |
| Expanded pilot (10-20 salons) | Oct 15 - Nov 15, 2026 | Onboard before the December rush |
| **December stress test** | Dec 1-31, 2026 | **Yaya proves its value during highest-revenue month** |
| Public launch | Jan 2027 | Launch when pain is fresh: "Remember how chaotic December was? Never again." |

### 3.3 Why January 2027 Is the Ideal Public Launch

January is when salon owners:
1. **Feel the pain most acutely** — December was chaos, January revenue dropped 40-50%
2. **Have time to evaluate new tools** — workload is at annual minimum
3. **Are setting new year resolutions** — "This year I'm going to get organized"
4. **Can see December's data** (if they were in the pilot) — "Look how much you actually sold in December. Was it what you expected?"

**The marketing message writes itself:** "¿Cuánto vendiste en diciembre? ¿Estás segura? Yaya sabe exactamente."

## 4. Seasonal Product Features

### 4.1 December-Specific Value Propositions

Yaya's value proposition shifts dramatically during peak season:

**During quiet months (Jan-Apr, Jun-Sep):**
- "Track your daily sales without lifting a pen"
- "Know exactly how much clients owe you (fíos)"
- "Send your contador organized records"

**During peak months (Nov-Dec):**
- "Don't lose track when you're serving 25 clients a day instead of 12"
- "Know in real-time if you're on track for your December revenue goal"
- "Voice note your sales between clients — takes 5 seconds"
- "Daily WhatsApp summary at 9pm: how much you made today, this week, this month"

**Post-peak (January):**
- "Here's your complete December report. You made S/18,450 — your best month ever."
- "Your total 2026 revenue was S/96,000. Your best clients are..."
- "Share this report with your contador — your annual tax filing is half-done"

### 4.2 Seasonal Features Roadmap

| Feature | Season | Value |
|---------|--------|-------|
| Revenue goal tracking | Oct-Dec | "Set a December goal, track daily progress" |
| Rush-mode quick entry | Nov-Dec | Simplified voice commands: "Venta 50" (no confirmation Flow) |
| Year-end report generator | Jan | Complete annual financial summary |
| Slow-season analytics | Jan-Mar | "Your slowest day is Tuesday. Consider promotions." |
| Mother's Day prep alerts | Apr | "Last year you served 35 clients in Mother's Day week. Prepare supplies." |
| Appointment reminder integration | All | "Recordar a María su cita a las 3pm" |

### 4.3 The "December Report" as Viral Acquisition Tool

**Concept:** In January, offer a free "December Revenue Report" to any salon owner who records even one week of December sales in Yaya. This creates:
1. **Urgency** to adopt before December ends
2. **Tangible value** from the first interaction
3. **A shareable artifact** — salon owners show each other their reports
4. **Contador interest** — contadores see the report and want it for all their clients

## 5. Cultural Calendar Deep Dive: Peru's Salon-Relevant Events

### 5.1 Major Revenue Events

| Event | Date | Impact on Salons | Revenue Boost |
|-------|------|-----------------|---------------|
| Día de la Madre | 2nd Sunday of May | Massive — gifts, self-pampering, family events | +30-50% that week |
| Fiestas Patrias | July 28-29 | National pride events, parties, family gatherings | +15-25% |
| Halloween | Oct 31 | Growing trend in Lima, especially among young people | +10-15% |
| Graduaciones | Nov-Dec | High school and university graduations drive formal grooming | +20-30% |
| Navidad | Dec 24-25 | Family gatherings, church services, gift exchanges | Peak season |
| Año Nuevo | Dec 31 | Year's biggest party night — hair, nails, makeup all at max | Highest single day of year |
| Quinceañeros | Year-round (peak May-Nov) | 15th birthday celebrations — full salon services for birthday girl + family | Variable, significant |
| Weddings | May-Nov (peak season) | Bridal services, wedding party preparation | +15-20% |

### 5.2 Revenue Valleys

| Period | Why It's Slow | Typical Revenue Drop |
|--------|-------------|---------------------|
| January 1-31 | Post-holiday hangover, school expenses | -30-40% vs. December |
| Post-Easter (late April) | Travel week, religious observance | -15-20% vs. average |
| June (non-holiday weeks) | Mid-year lull, winter in Lima (cloudy, less social activity) | -10-15% |
| August (first 3 weeks) | No major holidays, winter continues | -10-15% |

### 5.3 Lima-Specific Factors

- **Garúa season (May-October):** Lima's famous coastal fog/drizzle reduces going-out behavior, but also creates demand for anti-frizz and moisture treatments
- **Summer (Dec-Mar):** Lima finally gets sun; beach culture drives waxing, highlights, and body treatments
- **Verano escolar (school summer, Dec 15 - Mar 1):** Families travel, some Lima salons see reduced traffic while beach-town salons boom

## 6. Competitive Timing Analysis

### 6.1 When Competitors Launch

Most LATAM SaaS launches happen:
- **Q1 (Jan-Mar):** Post-VC funding round, new year energy
- **Q3 (Jul-Sep):** Post-midyear strategy review

By launching Yaya's pilot in **June 2026** and public launch in **January 2027**, Andre avoids competing with a wave of Q1 launches and instead launches with a differentiated message backed by real December data.

### 6.2 The Data Moat Timing

If Yaya has 20+ salons recording transactions through December 2026, by January 2027 it has:
- Real Peruvian salon revenue data across a complete seasonal cycle
- Proof of December's actual revenue patterns (most salon owners don't know their exact numbers)
- A compelling story: "Our pilot users discovered they earned 15% more in December than they thought"
- Reference customers who can speak to Yaya's value during the hardest month

**No competitor entering in Q1 2027 can replicate 6 months of seasonal data.**

## 7. Revenue Forecasting Implications

### 7.1 Churn Risk by Season

Churn in salon SaaS is seasonal:
- **Highest churn months:** January-February (salon owners cut costs after December rush ends)
- **Lowest churn months:** October-November (nobody cancels right before their busiest period)

**Implication for Yaya's pricing:**
- Consider **annual prepayment discount** offered in September-October: "Pay for 12 months, get 2 free"

## 8. The "Seasonality as Product" Opportunity

### 8.1 Most Salon Owners Don't Know Their Own Numbers

From the ethnography (market/12), salon owners track sales in cuadernos (notebooks) or not at all. This means:
- They don't know their exact December revenue
- They can't compare months or years
- They don't know which services are most profitable
- They can't predict quiet months or plan accordingly

**Yaya's unique value: making the invisible visible.** After 6-12 months of data, Yaya can tell a salon owner:
- "December was your best month at S/18,450. January was your worst at S/7,200."
- "You average 14 clients on Saturdays and 8 on Tuesdays."
- "Your busiest week was the one before Navidad."
- "You have S/2,400 in uncollected fíos from clients who haven't returned in 30+ days."

### 8.2 Predictive Features (6-12 Months Post-Launch)

Once Yaya has a year of data across multiple salons:
- **Revenue prediction:** "Based on your pattern, December should bring S/16,000-20,000. Last year you made S/18,450."
- **Slow day optimization:** "Tuesdays are consistently 40% slower. Consider a Tuesday promotion: descuento de martes."
- **Supply planning:** "In December you use 2x your normal product supplies. Order early."
- **Cash flow forecasting:** "January will likely be S/7,000-8,000. Make sure you have 2 months of rent saved from December."

These predictive features are **impossible** for a salon owner with a notebook. They're only possible with consistent digital record-keeping — which is exactly what Yaya provides.
