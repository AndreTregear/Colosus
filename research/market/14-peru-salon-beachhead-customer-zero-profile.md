## 3. Customer Zero Persona

### 3.1 "Rosa" — The Ideal Customer Zero

Based on salon market data, micro-enterprise ethnography (market/12), and beachhead analysis (market/09):

| Attribute | Profile |
|---|---|
| **Name archetype** | Rosa / María / Carmen / Lucía |
| **Age** | 28-45 |
| **Gender** | Female (80-85% of salon owners) |
| **Location** | Lima, middle-income district (Jesús María, Lince, Pueblo Libre, Surquillo, Los Olivos) |
| **Salon type** | 1-3 chair salon, 1-2 employees (possibly owner + 1 stylist) |
| **Monthly revenue** | S/6,000-12,000 ($1,600-3,200) |
| **Daily transactions** | 8-18 |
| **Current record-keeping** | Cuaderno (notebook) + WhatsApp messages to herself + calculator |
| **Phone** | Android (Samsung or Xiaomi), data plan |
| **WhatsApp usage** | Heavy — business and personal, 50+ messages/day |
| **Digital literacy** | Uses Yape, WhatsApp, Facebook/Instagram; doesn't use spreadsheets |
| **Pain points** | Doesn't know daily profit, loses track of fíos, messy notebook, hates month-end accounting |
| **Education** | Cosmetology school certificate, possibly incomplete university |
| **Formality** | Has RUC (persona natural), emits boletas occasionally when clients request |
| **Financial access** | BCP or Interbank savings account, uses Yape heavily |
| **Contador relationship** | Pays S/150-250/month for basic MYPE accounting + RUS tax filing |

### 3.3 Why This Profile Is Perfect for Yaya

1. **High transaction volume (8-18/day):** Enough data to prove value from Day 1
2. **Mix of payment methods:** Cash + Yape + Plin + card = needs to track them all
3. **Fío (credit) tracking:** The #1 pain point — loses track of who owes what
4. **WhatsApp-native:** Already uses WhatsApp for everything, zero adoption friction
5. **Moderate digital literacy:** Can use Yape but can't use Excel — Yaya fills the gap
6. **Contador relationship:** Can validate Yaya's data and provide feedback channel
7. **Recurring revenue potential:** Will pay S/15-30/month if it saves time on month-end
8. **Word-of-mouth potential:** Salon owners know other salon owners (same supply chains)

## 4. Customer Zero Recruitment Playbook

### 4.1 Finding Candidates

**Method 1: Contador Channel (Highest Quality)**
- Contact 3-5 independent contadores in Lima middle-income districts
- Ask: "Do you have a salon owner client who uses WhatsApp heavily and complains about record-keeping?"
- The contador pre-qualifies the candidate AND provides trusted introduction

**Method 2: Direct Outreach (Facebook/Instagram)**
- Search Lima salon Facebook pages and Instagram accounts
- Filter for: active posting, WhatsApp contact listed, 1-3 chair operation
- Message: "Estamos probando una app gratuita que te ayuda a llevar tus cuentas por WhatsApp. ¿Te interesaría probarla?"

**Method 3: Physical Walk-In**
- Visit salons in target districts
- Look for: cuaderno on counter, Yape QR visible, owner present
- Introduce Yaya concept directly — "¿Llevas tus cuentas a mano? Te puedo mostrar algo que te va a encantar"

**Method 4: WhatsApp Group Mining**
- Lima has WhatsApp groups for salon supply sharing/networking
- Join via supplier contacts or Facebook groups
- Natural introduction after contributing value to group

### 4.2 Screening Criteria

| Must Have | Nice to Have | Disqualifier |
|---|---|---|
| Salon in Lima | In target district | Already uses salon management software |
| WhatsApp daily user | Active WhatsApp Business | Fewer than 5 daily transactions |
| 8+ transactions/day | Mix of cash + digital payments | No smartphone or very old Android |
| Willing to try for 2 weeks | Has a contador | Plans to close or relocate soon |
| Speaks clearly in voice notes | Refers friends easily | Inconsistent business hours |

### 4.3 Incentive Structure

- **Week 1-2:** Free service, daily check-ins
- **Week 3-4:** If engaged, offer 6 months free service
- **Ongoing:** Acknowledged as "founding user" — input on features
- **Financial:** No cash payment — this creates transactional bias. The value of Yaya itself must be the incentive.
- **Social proof:** With permission, use their story as testimonial

### 4.4 Onboarding Script (In-Person, 15 minutes)

```
1. "Rosa, te voy a enseñar algo que va a cambiar cómo llevas tus cuentas.
    Todo por WhatsApp, sin descargar nada."

2. "Agrega este número a tus contactos: [Yaya number]"

3. "Mándale un mensaje: 'Hola'"
   → Yaya responds with welcome + onboarding Flow

4. [Onboarding Flow: name, tipo de negocio: salón, ¿cuántas sillas?]

5. "Ahora, grabá un audio como si me estuvieras contando una venta.
    Por ejemplo: 'Vendí un corte por treinta y cinco soles'"
   → Yaya transcribes → Confirmation Flow → "¡Lo registró! 🎉"

6. "Cada vez que vendas algo, grabá un audio así.
    Al final del día, preguntále '¿cuánto vendí hoy?' y te da el resumen."

7. "Si le fías a alguien, decile: 'Le fié treinta soles a Carmen.'
    Después podés preguntar '¿quién me debe?'"

8. "¿Tenés alguna pregunta?"

9. "Te voy a escribir todos los días para saber cómo te va.
    Cualquier problema, me avisás."
```

**Total onboarding time:** <15 minutes  
**Technical requirement:** Customer Zero just needs to add a WhatsApp contact and send a message  
**Success criteria:** Customer Zero records first real transaction on Day 1

## 5. Customer Zero Success Metrics

### 5.1 Daily Tracking (Weeks 9-10)

| Metric | Target | How to Measure |
|---|---|---|
| Voice notes sent per day | ≥5 | Database: message count by user |
| Transactions recorded per day | ≥5 | Database: transaction count |
| Voice vs. text ratio | >60% voice | Message type analysis |
| Flow confirmation rate | >85% | Flow completion analytics |
| Query usage ("¿cuánto vendí?") | ≥1/day | Intent log |
| Errors requiring correction | <15% | Correction log |
| Response time experienced | <5 seconds | Webhook timing logs |
| Daily summary opened | >80% | Template delivery + read receipts |

### 5.2 Weekly Check-In Questions

1. "¿Cuántas veces usaste Yaya hoy?" (validates self-reported vs. actual)
2. "¿Algo no entendió bien?" (identifies accuracy issues)
3. "¿Seguís usando el cuaderno también?" (dual-tracking indicates low trust)
4. "¿Le contaste a alguien?" (organic referral signal)
5. "¿Qué le falta?" (feature requests)
6. "Del 1 al 10, ¿se lo recomendarías a una amiga?" (NPS proxy)

### 5.3 Success Definition

**Customer Zero is a success if after 2 weeks:**
- Recording ≥5 transactions daily via voice (not reverting to notebook)
- Checking daily summary regularly
- Would recommend to a friend (NPS ≥ 8)
- Has stopped using notebook for tracked items OR uses Yaya as primary
- Has mentioned Yaya to at least 1 other salon owner

**Customer Zero is failing if:**
- Drops below 3 transactions/day for 3+ consecutive days
- Asks "can I just text instead?" consistently (voice isn't working)
- Reports more corrections than confirmations
- Returns to notebook exclusively

## 7. Risk Analysis: What Could Go Wrong with Customer Zero

| Risk | Probability | Impact | Mitigation |
|---|---|---|---|
| Customer Zero is too polite to give honest feedback | High | Medium | Observe behavior data (usage frequency), not just words |
| Customer Zero's salon is atypically quiet/noisy | Medium | Medium | Recruit backup candidate in different environment |
| Customer Zero's Spanish has unusual dialect features | Low | High | Adjust Whisper initial_prompt; recruit from Lima mainstream |
| Customer Zero loses interest after novelty wears off | Medium | High | Daily check-ins; demonstrate clear value (monthly report) |
| Customer Zero shares negative review with peers | Low | High | Fix issues immediately; over-communicate; be honest about beta status |
| Contador sees Yaya as threat | Low | Medium | Frame as tool that makes their job easier, not replaces them |
| Phone compatibility issues (old Android) | Low | Medium | Test on same phone model before recruiting |

| Transaction complexity | Low (service + price) | Medium (multiple items) | High (tables, tips, recipes) | Medium (inventory) |

| Owner availability for testing | High | Medium | Low (always busy cooking) | Medium |
| Female owner percentage | 80%+ | 50% | 30% | 50% |

**Salons win on:** transaction simplicity, voice note naturalness, referral density, and owner availability. Once Yaya proves itself in salons, expanding to bodegas (next) is a vocabulary adjustment, not an architecture change.

## 9. Competitive Moat from Customer Zero

Every Customer Zero interaction generates defensible data:

1. **Peruvian salon vocabulary model:** Whisper initial_prompt refined by real usage → competitors starting from scratch won't have this
2. **Transaction patterns:** Peak hours, average tickets, payment method mix → enables predictive features
3. **Fío behavior data:** Credit patterns, default rates, payment timing → foundation for future lending products
4. **User interaction preferences:** How long voice notes are, what queries they ask, when they engage → UX optimization data
5. **Noise profile library:** Real salon acoustic samples → training data for accuracy improvement

This is the beginning of the data moat described in strategy/16.

## 10. Key Takeaways

1. **INEI 2025 confirms salon sector health:** 1,100-1,350 new salons registered per quarter in Peru, 18% quarterly growth. The beachhead is not shrinking.

2. **Customer Zero is a medium formal salon in a Lima middle-income district:** 8-18 daily transactions, S/6,000-12,000/month revenue, owner is a WhatsApp-native woman aged 28-45 who tracks everything in a notebook.

3. **Recruitment via contador is highest quality:** Pre-qualified, trusted introduction, built-in feedback loop.

4. **Onboarding takes 15 minutes and zero technical setup:** Add WhatsApp contact → send "Hola" → complete Flow → record first transaction. Done.

5. **Success = 5+ voice notes per day for 2 weeks.** If Customer Zero does this, Yaya has product-market fit signal. If not, iterate before scaling.

6. **The salon beachhead feeds directly into multi-vertical expansion.** Salons are the simplest case; proving it here unlocks bodegas, tiendas, and restaurants with minimal additional development.
