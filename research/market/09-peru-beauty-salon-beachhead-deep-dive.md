## 1. Executive Summary

Peru's beauty and personal care industry closed 2025 with **S/10,000 million** (US$2,936M) in sales — a 7% growth rate that is projected to continue into 2026 at **S/10,850 million**. The sector represents 1% of Peru's GDP. With **15 million heavy users**, 120 new companies entering in 2025 alone, and 530 new brands launched, the industry is booming. But here's the critical insight for Yaya: the industry is supply-rich and management-poor. Beauty businesses are multiplying, but the tools to run them haven't kept up. The salon scheduling software market in LATAM grew 180% between 2020-2025, yet penetration among Peruvian micro-salons remains near zero.

This document provides the market data Andre needs to validate — or refine — the beauty salon beachhead strategy.

## 3. The Beauty Salon Business in Peru

### 3.1 Estimating the Salon Universe

Peru doesn't publish official salon counts, but we can triangulate:

- **INEI formal enterprise data:** Beauty services fall under CIIU 9602 (peluquería y otros tratamientos de belleza). Estimated 80,000-120,000 formal + informal beauty businesses nationwide.
- **Lima concentration:** ~29.5% of MYPEs are in Lima → ~24,000-35,000 beauty businesses in Lima
- **Industry context:** With S/10B in industry revenue and beauty services being a significant portion (services revenue separate from product sales), the salon market alone is likely S/3-5B
- **Comparison:** Mexico has ~300,000+ salons; Colombia ~150,000+. Peru at ~100,000 is proportionate to population.

### 3.3 The Pain Points: Why Salons Need Yaya

**Pain 1: No-shows and last-minute cancellations**
- Industry average no-show rate: 20-30% for salons without reminder systems
- Software-based reminders reduce no-shows by **50-67%** (AgendaPro data)
- For a salon with 10 appointments/day at S/50 average: no-shows cost **S/100-150/day = S/3,000-4,500/month**
- Reducing no-shows by 50% = **S/1,500-2,250/month recovered** — 30-100× the S/49 subscription cost

**Pain 2: Manual scheduling chaos**
- Salons using paper agendas suffer **8-12 scheduling conflicts per week** (AgendaPro study)
- Average administrative time: 3-4 hours/day on scheduling, confirmations, follow-ups
- With automation: 30 minutes/day

**Pain 3: No client history or follow-up**
- 75.3% keep no records → no client preferences, no service history, no re-engagement
- Client who doesn't return within 6 weeks has 40% chance of never returning
- Automated re-engagement messages increase repeat visits by 22-35%

**Pain 4: Cash flow invisibility**
- No daily revenue tracking, no expense tracking
- "How much did I make today?" is answered by counting cash, not consulting data
- Cash-heavy businesses underreport income, miss optimization opportunities

### 5.2 Expansion Within the Beauty Ecosystem

The beauty industry has natural network effects:

```
Phase 1: Individual salons
  → Beauty supply distributors (L'Oréal, Wella, local distributors)
  → Beauty schools and training academies
  → Instagram/TikTok beauty influencers
  → Salon equipment providers

Phase 2: Adjacent beauty businesses
  → Nail salons (similar workflow)
  → Spas and wellness centers
  → Barbershops (male market)
  → Aesthetic clinics (higher ARPU, facial treatment growing 10.1%)

Phase 3: Cross-vertical from beauty
  → Restaurants (same scheduling + payment needs)
  → Medical/dental clinics (appointment-heavy)
  → Fitness studios/gyms (subscription + scheduling)
```

### 5.3 The Supply Chain Opportunity

**The S/10B beauty industry has a massive B2B layer:**
- Imports grew 11% in 2025, representing 77% of the market
- Colombia is Peru's top beauty product supplier (US$685M, +15%)
- China growing fast (US$217M, +43%)
- 75% of new companies import below US$100K FOB

Yaya can eventually serve the **supply chain** — helping salon owners order products from distributors via WhatsApp, track inventory of professional products, and reorder automatically. This B2B layer has higher ARPU and lower churn.
