## 1. Executive Summary

Latin America's venture capital ecosystem in 2025 showed a selective recovery: US$4.126 billion deployed across only 681 rounds — 13.8% more capital than 2024 but the fewest deals since 2017. The signal is unambiguous: investors are writing larger checks to fewer companies with stronger traction. For Yaya Platform, this means the fundraising environment rewards exactly what we're building — a revenue-generating, capital-efficient product with clear unit economics — while punishing the kind of pre-revenue idea-stage companies that flourished in 2021.

## 3. What Investors Want in 2026

Based on the Cuantico VP report and startup VC analysis, investors are applying increasingly stringent criteria:

### 3.1 Non-Negotiable Requirements

1. **Founder Track Record:** Repeat and serial entrepreneurs accounted for 42% of total capital deployed in 2023-2024 (vs. 23% in 2021). First-time founders face a significant disadvantage unless they compensate with exceptional traction.

2. **Revenue Traction:** Repeatable sales matter more than growth projections. Investors want to see real revenue, not just users.

3. **Unit Economics:** Strong margins and controlled customer acquisition costs are non-negotiable. The era of "grow at all costs" is definitively over.

4. **Governance:** Clean cap tables, formal reporting, and clear equity structures reduce diligence friction.

### 3.2 Preferred Sectors

- Fintech remains the dominant sector (61% of capital)
- SaaS consolidated as second (13% of both deals and capital)
- AI is the fastest-growing investment category by deal count (20 deals in 2025)
- The intersection of AI + SaaS + Fintech is the sweet spot

### 3.3 Exit Landscape Improving

Exit activity surged to US$4.9 billion across 63 transactions in 2025, up from US$1.8 billion in 2024. Average exit size rose from US$29M to US$77.8M. This is the third-best year on record, improving LP confidence and future fund deployment.

## 5. Peru's VC Ecosystem — Realistic Assessment

### 5.1 Current State

Peru captured only US$35M across 8 deals in 2025 — 0.8% of regional VC. This makes Peru one of the smallest VC markets in LATAM.

**Notable Peru-based funded startups:**
- Kaudal (Education, undisclosed amount, May 2025)
- YaVendió (WhatsApp commerce, $850K seed)
- Crehana (EdTech, earlier rounds)

### 5.2 Local Investor Landscape

Peru's venture ecosystem is nascent but growing:
- **Angel investors:** Small but active network (Lima Angels, Startup Peru alumni)
- **Government programs:** Innóvate Perú, Startup Peru, PRODUCE programs
- **Regional funds investing in Peru:** Magma Partners (Chile), ALLVP (Mexico), Angel Ventures (Mexico)
- **Global funds active in LATAM:** Y Combinator, Sequoia (via Enter), Founders Fund, 500 Startups LATAM

### 5.3 Fundraising Strategy Implications

**Option A: Raise in Peru** — Very limited. Only 8 deals total in 2025. Local angel rounds of $50K-$200K are possible but won't fund significant growth.

**Option B: Raise from LATAM-focused funds based elsewhere** — More realistic. Colombian and Chilean funds have higher deal activity and invest cross-border.

**Option C: Apply to Y Combinator or similar accelerators** — Highest potential multiplier. A Peru-based AI+WhatsApp+SMB play would be novel in YC's portfolio. The "conversational ERP" category is genuinely new.

**Option D: Seedstrapping** — Most aligned with current resources. Build to revenue with existing infrastructure (c.yaya.sh), prove unit economics, then raise from a position of strength. The Cuantico VP data suggests this is exactly what investors want: "Revenue traction matters more than growth projections."
