## 3. WhatsApp as Full-Funnel Commerce Platform (2026)

### 3.1 The Funnel Paradigm Shift

By 2026, WhatsApp has evolved from a support/notification channel to a **full-funnel commerce layer**. Key shift: conversations replace landing pages as the highest-intent conversion path.

**Why it works:**
- No page transitions — user stays in a single conversational thread
- Context is preserved and compounds with each message
- Questions answered in real-time, objections resolved conversationally
- Awareness → Consideration → Conversion can happen in minutes, not days

### 3.2 Click-to-WhatsApp Ads (CTWA)

- Convert up to **3× better** than standard ad formats (Interakt)
- Businesses route warm traffic directly to WhatsApp instead of landing pages
- Meta launched **purchase optimization** for CTWA — Meta's algorithm optimizes for in-chat purchases
- Benchmark cost per conversation: €1.50-8.00 depending on industry

### 3.3 Cart/Abandonment Recovery

- WhatsApp cart recovery achieves **18-25% recovery rate** (Interakt)
- **25-30% cart recovery lift** vs. email (MarketingAgent case study)
- Key driver: contextual reminders that reference prior conversation feel helpful, not promotional
- Within 30 minutes of abandonment is the optimal timing

### 3.4 The AI + Human Orchestration Model

Best-performing WhatsApp commerce businesses in 2026 combine:
- **AI chatbots** for scale: handle 70-80% of inbound queries, FAQs, product recommendations
- **Human agents** for closure: step in when buying signals appear or complexity increases
- **Response SLA under 5 minutes** for human handoffs
- Result: 10-25% conversion rates on automated flows

## 7. WhatsApp Quality & Scaling Mechanics

### 7.1 Meta Quality Rating System

Meta now uses a **portfolio-based sending limit** (not per-number). Key metrics:
- **Quality Rating** (Green/Yellow/Red) determines sending capacity
- **Block-to-Read Ratio** is the early warning indicator
- **Scaling Velocity**: Qualified accounts can upgrade tiers in 6 hours (down from 24)
- From 1,000 → 10,000 → 100,000 messages/day with consistent quality

### 7.2 Best Practices for Maintaining Quality

- Low frequency, high relevance messaging (not spam)
- Keep opt-out rates below 0.2%
- Maintain block rates near zero
- Build sending volume continuously, not in spikes
- Segment users and personalize messages

### 7.3 Implications for Yaya

Yaya's model is inherently high-quality from Meta's perspective:
- Messages are **requested by the user** (they initiate most conversations)
- Content is **highly relevant** (their own business data)
- Frequency is **moderate and consistent** (daily summary, not spam blasts)
- The conversational nature means high reply rates (positive quality signal)
