## Executive Summary

Latin American venture capital has entered a "post-correction" phase. Total VC funding reached $4.1B in 2025 (up 13.8% YoY), with 58% of investors expressing medium-to-high confidence for 2026. Fintech still dominates (61% of capital), but AI is the emerging theme. For Yaya, the landscape offers a clear fundraising path: start seedstrapped, demonstrate unit economics with 50-100 users, then approach the wave of LATAM-focused AI funds entering the market.

## 1. The Numbers: LATAM VC in 2025-2026

### Headline Metrics
- **Total LATAM VC funding 2025:** $4.1 billion (up from $3.6B in 2024, +13.8% YoY)
- **Number of deals:** Slightly declined vs. 2024 (larger average round sizes)
- **LATAM VC Confidence Index:** 58% medium-to-high for next 6-18 months
- **47.2% of investors planning to increase activity in 2026**

### Market Concentration
- **Brazil:** $2.0B (52.9%) across 363 deals
- **Mexico:** $980M (25.5%) across 86 deals — ascending rapidly
- **Rest of LATAM:** $1.12B (21.6%)
- Key insight: Brazil's dominance is declining relative to Mexico's ascent
- **Arbitrage opportunity:** Mexico valuations 20-30% lower than Brazil for comparable traction

### Sector Breakdown
- **Fintech:** 29% of deals but **61% of total capital** — still the dominant theme
- **AI/Data:** Growing rapidly but hasn't yet captured fintech-level capital
- **Enterprise SaaS:** Steady growth, especially at Series A/B
- **Healthtech:** Emerging strong (Eden $22M, Nilo $2.9M, Avedian $2M)

## 4. The "Post-Correction" Thesis: What It Means for Yaya

### The Market Cycle
1. **2021:** Bubble — inflated valuations, easy money
2. **2022-2024:** Correction — funding down 70%+, startups dying
3. **2025-2026:** Recovery — higher structural baseline than pre-2020, but disciplined

### What's Changed
- **Capital efficiency is king.** Investors want to see unit economics, not just growth.
- **Revenue matters earlier.** Even seed-stage companies are expected to show paying customers.
- **AI is the narrative driver** but fintech captures the capital. Intersection of both = sweet spot.
- **Andean region is underrepresented** — ALIVE Ventures closing a $55M Andean-focused fund signals growing interest.

### Yaya's Positioning
Yaya sits at the intersection of multiple hot themes:
- ✅ **AI-powered** (voice recognition, NLP, financial automation)
- ✅ **WhatsApp-native** (validated by Vambe's $14M raise)
- ✅ **Fintech-adjacent** (transaction recording, e-invoicing, payment collection)
- ✅ **Impact thesis** (financial inclusion for informal micro-enterprises)
- ✅ **Andean market** (underserved, growing investor interest via ALIVE Ventures)
- ✅ **Vertical-specific** (beauty salons → clear beachhead)

## 6. Competitive Benchmarks for Fundraising Narrative

### "We're Vambe for micro-enterprises"
- Vambe raised $14M at ~$50-70M valuation with 1,700 implementations
- Vambe targets mid-market B2C; Yaya targets micro-enterprise B2B
- If Yaya reaches 500 implementations, a comparable narrative could support a $5-10M seed

### "We're Vyapar for Peru"
- Vyapar (India) reached 10M+ users with a WhatsApp-first bookkeeping app for micro-enterprises
- India's informal economy is structurally similar to Peru's
- Vyapar's scale proves the TAM exists

### "Financial inclusion through conversational AI"
- Quipu (Colombia) raised $1.1M pre-Series A building credit scoring for informal economy
- Yaya's financial transaction data could feed similar credit-scoring models
- Impact investors (ALIVE Ventures, IDB Lab) are actively funding this thesis
